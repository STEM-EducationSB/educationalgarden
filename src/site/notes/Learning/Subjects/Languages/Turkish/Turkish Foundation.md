## 7. Verbs (Highly Regular)

Verbs are built like math formulas.

### Infinitive

`gelmek = to come`

### Present Continuous

`geliyorum = I am coming geliyorsun = you are coming`

Tense + person are **suffixes**, not separate words.

Irregular verbs? Very few.

---

## 8. Sentence Structure

Basic order:

`Subject + Object + Verb`

Example:

`Ben elma yiyorum. I apple eat. = I am eating an apple.`

You can rearrange words for emphasis, but **the verb usually comes last**.

## 9. Vocabulary Sources

Modern Turkish vocabulary comes from:

- **Turkic roots** (core words)
    
- **Arabic & Persian** (historical influence)
    
- **French** (modern concepts)
    
- **English** (technology)
    

However, grammar remains **purely Turkic**.

---

## 10. Difficulty Assessment (Honest)

For an English speaker:

- **Pronunciation:** Easy
    
- **Spelling:** Very easy
    
- **Grammar:** Medium (new logic, but consistent)
    
- **Vocabulary:** Medium (few cognates)
    

Turkish is **hard at first, easy later**. Once the system clicks, progress accelerates.

---

## 11. Why Turkish Is Worth Learning

- Extremely **logical grammar**
    
- High **return on effort**
    
- Gateway to **other Turkic languages**
    
- Strong cultural, historical, and geopolitical relevance
    

If you like **systems, rules, and structure**, Turkish will suit you.
