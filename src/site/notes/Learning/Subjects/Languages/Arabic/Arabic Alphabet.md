## How Arabic Letter Forms Work (Very Important)

Arabic letters change shape depending on **position in a word**:

- **Isolated** – letter stands alone
    
- **Initial** – at the **start** of a word
    
- **Medial** – in the **middle**, connected on both sides
    
- **Final** – at the **end** of a word
    

⚠️ Some letters **do not connect to the left**, so they **never have medial or initial-connecting forms**.

## Letters That DO NOT Connect to the Left

These **break the word** visually:

> **ا د ذ ر ز و**

They **only have isolated & final forms**.

---

## Special Notes (Tell-it-like-it-is)

- **ع (ʿAyn)** and **ح (Ḥāʾ)** are the hardest for English speakers — mastery requires throat control.
    
- **Emphatic letters** (ص ض ط ظ ق) change nearby vowels — Arabic pronunciation is **not just letters, but resonance**.
    
- Arabic writing is **phonemic** — once you learn sounds, spelling is extremely logical.
