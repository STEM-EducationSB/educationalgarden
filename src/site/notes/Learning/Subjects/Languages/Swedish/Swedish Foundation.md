## 1. Language Overview

- **Family**: Indo-European → Germanic → **North Germanic**
    
- **Closest relatives**: Norwegian (very close), Danish (written similar, spoken harder)
    
- **Script**: Latin + **Å, Ä, Ö**
    
- **Difficulty (for English speakers)**: **Easy–Medium**
    
- **Strength**: Logical grammar, limited verb conjugation
    
- **Trap**: Pronunciation, pitch accent, and word order
    

---

## 2. Swedish Alphabet & Special Letters

Swedish uses 29 letters:

**A–Z + Å Ä Ö**

|Letter|Approx. Sound|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Å|“aw” in _law_|**år** (year)|
|Ä|“a” in _cat_|**är** (is/are)|
|Ö|“ur” in _hurt_ (rounded)|**öl** (beer)|

> **Rule**: Å, Ä, Ö are **full letters**, not accents. Alphabetical order ends in **… X Y Z Å Ä Ö**.

---

## 3. Pronunciation Core (Critical)

Swedish pronunciation matters more than spelling.

### Vowels (long vs short)

- Vowel length changes meaning
    
    - **tak** (roof) vs **tack** (thanks)
        

### Pitch Accent (Advanced but real)

- Two tones (Accent 1 & Accent 2)
    
- Changes meaning:
    
    - **anden** = _the duck_ / _the spirit_ (tone-dependent)
        

> You don’t need perfection early, but **exposure is mandatory**.

---

## 4. Articles & Gender System

Swedish has **two grammatical genders**:

|Gender|Article|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Common (≈75%)|**en**|**en bok** (a book)|
|Neuter|**ett**|**ett hus** (a house)|

> Learn nouns **with their article**. Non-negotiable.

---

## 5. Definite Form (Key Feature)

Swedish attaches “the” to the **end** of the noun.

|Indefinite|Definite|
|---|---|
|en bok|boken (the book)|
|ett hus|huset (the house)|

With adjectives:

- **den / det / de + adjective + noun-definite**
    

Example:

- **den röda boken** = the red book
    

---

## 6. Plurals (Patterns, Not Chaos)

Plural endings vary, but patterns exist:

|Ending|Example|
|---|---|
|-or|en flicka → flickor|
|-ar|en bil → bilar|
|-er|en stad → städer|
|-n|ett äpple → äpplen|
|no change|ett hus → hus|

## 8. Verbs (Simple, Clean System)

No verb conjugation by person.

|Infinitive|Present|Past|
|---|---|---|
|att tala (to speak)|talar|talade|
|att vara (to be)|är|var|

Example:

- **Jag talar svenska.** (I speak Swedish)
    
- **Vi är här.** (We are here)
    

---

## 9. Word Order (Very Important)

Swedish follows **V2 (Verb-Second)** rule.

### Basic sentence

> **Subject – Verb – Object**

- Jag äter bröd. (I eat bread.)
    

### When sentence starts with time/place

> **Time/Place – Verb – Subject**

- **Idag äter jag bröd.**
    
- **I Sverige talar man svenska.**
    

Break this rule and you will sound wrong instantly.

---

## 10. Questions & Negation

### Yes/No Questions

- Verb first:
    
    - **Är du här?** (Are you here?)
        

### Negation

- **inte** (not) usually after verb:
    
    - Jag talar **inte** svenska.

## 12. Core Survival Phrases

- **Hej!** – Hi
    
- **Tack!** – Thanks
    
- **Varsågod** – You’re welcome
    
- **Jag förstår inte.** – I don’t understand
    
- **Kan du hjälpa mig?** – Can you help me?
    
- **Jag lär mig svenska.** – I am learning Swedish
    

---

## 13. Learning Strategy (Tell-It-Like-It-Is)

1. **Pronunciation first** (listen daily)
    
2. Learn nouns **with en/ett**
    
3. Drill **V2 word order**
    
4. Shadow native audio
    
5. Read simple Swedish early (children’s news, subtitles)
    

If you do this, Swedish will **feel logical, not heavy**.
