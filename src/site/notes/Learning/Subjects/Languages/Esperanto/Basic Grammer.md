# **Esperanto: Full Grammar Tutorial**

Esperanto’s strength is **perfect consistency**. No silent letters, no irregular verbs, no exceptions. If you know the rules once, they apply everywhere.

---

# **1. Alphabet & Pronunciation (Zero Exceptions)**

Esperanto uses **28 letters**, all pronounced exactly as written.

**Vowels:** a, e, i, o, u  
Always short, clean, open.

**Consonants with diacritics:**  
ĉ (ch), ĝ (j in “jump”), ĥ (kh), ĵ (zh in “measure”), ŝ (sh), ŭ (w in “cow”).

**Examples:**

- ĉokolado – chocolate
    
- ŝipo – ship
    
- ĵurnalo – journal
    

---

# **2. Word Endings: The Core Logic of Esperanto**

Everything is built around endings.

|Type|Ending|Example|Meaning|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Noun**|**-o**|domo|house|
|**Adjective**|**-a**|bela|beautiful|
|**Adverb**|**-e**|rapide|quickly|
|**Verb (infinitive)**|**-i**|fari|to do|

Plural: **add -j** → domoj (houses), belaj (beautiful, plural)
Accusative (object): **add -n** → domon (a house as an object)

Plural + Accusative: **-jn** → domojn

---

# **3. The Accusative Case (-n)**

Esperanto marks the **direct object** with **-n**.

- Mi vidas la hundon.  
    I see the dog.
    

Accusative also marks **movement toward** something:

- Mi iras hejmen.  
    I am going home (towards home).
    

---

# **4. Verbs: Zero Irregularity**

Verbs never change for person:  
mi estas, vi estas, ili estas — always the same ending.

### **Verb Tenses**

|Tense|Ending|Example (vid-)|Meaning|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Present**|-as|vidas|see|
|**Past**|-is|vidis|saw|
|**Future**|-os|vidos|will see|
|**Conditional**|-us|vidus|would see|
|**Volitive/Imperative**|-u|vidu|see (command/wish)|
|**Infinitive**|-i|vidi|to see|

---

# **5. Articles & Determiners**

Only **one article**: **la** (the).  
No indefinite article (a/an).

- domo → a house
    
- la domo → the house
    

Pronouns:

| Pronoun | Meaning                 |
| ------- | ----------------------- |
| mi      | I                       |
| vi      | you                     |
| li      | he                      |
| ŝi      | she                     |
| ĝi      | it                      |
| ni      | we                      |
| ili     | they                    |
| oni     | one / people in general |

Possessives add **-a**:

- mia libro – my book
    
- ilia domo – their house
    

---

# **6. Adjectives**

Adjectives always end in **-a** and agree with the noun in **number** and **accusative**:

- bela domo
    
- belaj domoj
    
- belan domon
    
- belajn domojn
    

Order is flexible:

- bela domo = domo bela
    

Both valid.

---

# **7. Adverbs**

Always end in **-e**.

Examples:

- rapide – quickly
    
- bone – well
    
- hejme – at home
    

Create adverbs from adjectives by replacing **-a** → **-e**:

bela → bele (beautifully)

---

# **8. Word Formation (Esperanto’s Superpower)**

Esperanto is **modular**. You can build new words like LEGO blocks using:

### **Prefixes**

- mal- = opposite  
    bela (beautiful) → malbela (ugly)
    
- re- = again  
    legi (to read) → relegi (to reread)
    

### **Suffixes**

Some important ones:

|Suffix|Meaning|Example|
|---|---|---|
|-et-|small|dometo – small house|
|-eg-|big|domego – mansion|
|-in-|female|katino – female cat|
|-ej-|place|lernejo – school|
|-ul-|person with trait|belulo – handsome person|
|-ist-|profession|kuracisto – doctor|
|-em-|tendency|laborema – hard-working|
|-ebl-|possible|farebla – doable|

You can mix them:

- laboremegi – work extremely hard
    
- manĝebla – edible
    
- belulineto – cute little pretty girl
    

---

# **9. Plurals**

Add **-j**.

- libro → libroj
    
- granda → grandaj
    

Accusative plural ends with **-jn**:

- grandajn librojn
    

---

# **10. Word Order**

**Flexible**, but default is:

**Subject – Verb – Object**

- Mi amas vin.  
    I love you.
    

But Esperanto allows:

- Vin mi amas.
    
- Mi vin amas.
    

Everything stays clear because of the **-n** ending on objects.

---

# **11. Prepositions**

Always followed by **nominative** unless expressing motion.

Key prepositions:

- al – to
    
- de – from/of
    
- por – for
    
- kun – with
    
- sen – without
    
- sur – on
    
- en – in
    

With motion:

- Mi iras en la domon.  
    I go into the house.
    

Without motion:

- Mi estas en la domo.  
    I am in the house.
    

---

# **12. Questions**

Formed with **ĉu** (yes/no questions):

- Ĉu vi venos?  
    Will you come?
    

Wh- questions use the **ki-** words:

|Esperanto|Meaning|
|---|---|
|kiu|who / which|
|kio|what|
|kie|where|
|kiam|when|
|kial|why|
|kiel|how|
|kiom|how much/many|

---

# **13. Correlatives Chart (Most Powerful Part)**

Esperanto has a perfect grid of 45 “correlative” words.

Here’s the core:

|Meaning|Table Pattern|
|---|---|
|Question|ki-|
|Demonstrative|ti-|
|Indefinite|i-|
|Universal|ĉiu-|
|Negative|nen-|

Endings control meaning:

| Ending | Meaning    |
| ------ | ---------- |
| -o     | thing      |
| -u     | person     |
| -e     | place      |
| -a     | kind/type  |
| -am    | time       |
| -el    | manner     |
| -om    | quantity   |
| -al    | reason     |
| -es    | possession |

Example combos:

- kio – what
    
- tio – that
    
- ĉio – everything
    
- nenio – nothing
    
- kie – where
    
- tie – there
    
- ĉie – everywhere
    
- nenie – nowhere
    

---

# **14. Passive Voice**

Formed with **esti + participle**.

Participle endings:

|Type|Ending|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Present active|-ant-|vidanta – seeing|
|Past active|-int-|vidinta – having seen|
|Future active|-ont-|vidonta – about to see|
|Present passive|-at-|vidata – being seen|
|Past passive|-it-|vidita – seen|
|Future passive|-ot-|vidota – to be seen|

Example:

- La libro estas legata.  
    The book is being read.
    
- La libro estis legita.  
    The book was read.
    

---

# **15. Comparison**

- pli – more
    
- plej – most
    
- malpli – less
    
- malplej – least
    
- ol – than
    

Example:

- Li estas pli alta ol mi.  
    He is taller than me.
    

---

# **16. Negation**

Use **ne** in front of the verb.

- Mi ne venas.  
    I am not coming.
    

Double negatives are allowed and logical:

- Neniu neniam venas.  
    No one ever comes.
    

---

# **17. Useful Sentence Templates**

- Mi volas …  
    I want …
    
- Ĉu vi povas … ?  
    Can you …?
    
- Mi ne komprenas.  
    I don’t understand.
    
- Mi lernas Esperanton.  
    I am learning Esperanto.
    

---

# **18. Practice Mini-Text**

Mi estas Shatadru kaj mi lernas Esperanton.  
La lingvo estas tre regula kaj facile komprenebla.  
Mi volas paroli ĝin flue post kelkaj tagoj.  
Ĉiu tago mi praktikas novajn vortojn kaj frazojn.  
Baldaŭ mi povos skribi longajn tekstojn sen eraroj.

Translation:  
I am Shatadru and I am learning Esperanto.  
The language is very regular and easy to understand.  
I want to speak it fluently in a few days.  
Every day I practice new words and sentences.  
Soon I will be able to write long texts without mistakes.
