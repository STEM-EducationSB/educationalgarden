# Full Chinese (Mandarin) Grammar Tutorial for Very Beginners

Great — you want a complete starter guide. I’ll give you a compact, practical, and systematic tutorial that takes you from zero to confident beginner: pronunciation, essential grammar, core sentence patterns, particles, complements, common constructions, practice tasks, and a simple study plan. No fluff — just what you need to start making real sentences and understanding how Chinese works.

---

## 1) Quick orientation — what Mandarin looks like

- Spoken standard: **Mandarin (普通话 / Putonghua)**.
    
- Writing: **Simplified characters** (used in Mainland China) or **Traditional** (used in Taiwan, Hong Kong). This guide uses simplified characters but explains structure that applies to both.
    
- Romanization: **Pinyin** — must-learn for pronunciation. Every example shows characters + pinyin + English.
    

---

## 2) Absolute first things to learn (day 1)

1. **Pinyin initials & finals** (b, p, m, f, d, t, n, l, g, k, h, j, q, x, zh, ch, sh, r, z, c, s + finals a o e i u ü)
    
2. **Four tones (+ neutral/tone 0)** — tone shape matters. Practice minimal pairs: mā (妈) / má (麻) / mǎ (马) / mà (骂).
    
3. **Numbers 0–10** and basic pronouns: 我 wǒ (I), 你 nǐ (you), 他/她 tā (he/she), 我们 wǒmen (we).
    
4. **Basic verbs:** 是 shì (to be), 有 yǒu (to have), 去 qù (go), 来 lái (come), 看 kàn (see), 吃 chī (eat), 喝 hē (drink), 想 xiǎng (want/think), 会 huì (can/learned ability).
    

Practice: say “I am X”, “I have X”, “I want X”, “I can X”.

---

## 3) Word order (the core)

**Mandarin is largely S V O (Subject–Verb–Object).**  
Examples:

- 我( Subject ) 吃( Verb ) 苹果( Object )。  
    Wǒ chī píngguǒ. — I eat an apple.
    
- 她 看 书。 Tā kàn shū. — She reads a book.
    

Important: Time/manner/place adverbials usually go **before** the verb:

- 我 **昨天** 去 学校。 Wǒ zuótiān qù xuéxiào. — I went to school yesterday.
    
- 我 **慢慢地** 说。 Wǒ mànmàn de shuō. — I speak slowly.

## 4) Basic sentence components & patterns

### 4.1 Questions

- **Yes/no question:** add **吗 (ma)** at end.  
    你是老师吗？Nǐ shì lǎoshī ma? — Are you a teacher?
    
- **A-not-A** (confirm): 你去不去？Nǐ qù bù qù? — Are you going or not?
    
- **Question words:** 谁 shéi (who), 什么 shénme (what), 哪里/哪儿 nǎlǐ/nǎr (where), 什么时候 shénme shíhou (when), 怎么 zěnme (how), 为什么 wèishénme (why).
    
    - 你叫什么名字？Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi? — What’s your name?
        

### 4.2 Negation

- **不 (bù)** — general, present/future habitual: 我**不**吃。Wǒ bù chī. — I (do not/won’t) eat.
    
- **没 (méi)** — for completed actions (hadn't/didn't) and possession in past: 我**没**吃。Wǒ méi chī. — I didn’t eat. / 我**没有**钱。Wǒ méiyǒu qián. — I don’t have money.
    

### 4.3 The verb 是 (to be)

- Use **是** for linking nouns or titles:  
    他是老师。Tā shì lǎoshī. — He is a teacher.
    
- For adjectives, Chinese often drops 是 and uses the adjective as a verb with degree adverb:  
    他很高。Tā hěn gāo. — He is tall. (literally: He very tall)
    

### 4.4 Adjectives

- Can act like verbs: **我忙。Wǒ máng.** (I am busy.)
    
- Degree words: 很 (hěn), 太 (tài), 非常 (fēicháng), 不太 (bù tài).

## 5) Particles — tiny but crucial words

### 5.1 的 (de) — possession/attribution

- 我**的**书 wǒ de shū — my book
    
- 他是**中国的**老师。Tā shì Zhōngguó de lǎoshī. — He is a teacher from China.
    

### 5.2 了 (le) — aspect marker / change of state

- Action completed / new situation: 我吃**了**。Wǒ chī le. — I ate. / I have eaten.
    
- Placed after verb or at sentence end depending on nuance. (Tricky — see exercises.)
    

### 5.3 过 (guò) — experiential aspect

- 我去**过**北京。Wǒ qù guo Běijīng. — I have been to Beijing.
    

### 5.4 着 (zhe) — continuous state

- 他笑**着**。Tā xiào zhe. — He is smiling (state).
    

### 5.5 吗 (ma) — yes/no question marker (mentioned above)

### 5.6 呢 (ne) — follow-up / context question

- 你呢？Nǐ ne? — And you?
    

### 5.7 把 (bǎ) — disposal / focus on object being acted on

- 我把书放在桌子上。Wǒ bǎ shū fàng zài zhuōzi shàng. — I placed the book on the table.
    

### 5.8 被 (bèi) — passive voice

- 他被老师批评了。Tā bèi lǎoshī pīpíng le. — He was criticized by the teacher.
    

---

## 6) Measure words / classifiers

Mandarin uses a classifier between number/demonstrative and noun. The most common is **个 (gè)**.

- 一个苹果 yí ge píngguǒ — one apple  
    Common classifiers:
    
- 本 běn — for books (一本书)
    
- 张 zhāng — flat objects (一张纸)
    
- 只 zhī — animals, single of pair (一只猫)
    
- 双 shuāng — pair (一双鞋)
    

Learn: **number + classifier + noun**.

---

## 7) Modal verbs & ability

- 会 huì — learned ability / will (I can / will)  
    我会游泳。Wǒ huì yóuyǒng. — I can swim.
    
- 能 néng / 可以 kěyǐ — permission or capability  
    我能去吗？Wǒ néng qù ma? — Can I go?
    
- 要 yào — will / want (future intention)  
    我要喝水。Wǒ yào hē shuǐ. — I want to drink water.
    

---

## 8) Complements — result, direction, degree

- **Resultative complement:** 看见 kànjiàn (see + result = see successfully).  
    我看见他了。Wǒ kànjiàn tā le. — I saw him.
    
- **Directional complements:** 进/出/上/下/回 + 来/去 e.g., 走进来 zǒu jìnlái — walk in (towards speaker).
    
- **Degree complement:** 很/太/得 + adjective: 他跑得快。Tā pǎo de kuài. — He runs fast.
    

---

## 9) Relative clauses (modifying nouns)

Structure: **[clause] + 的 + noun**

- 我认识的老师。Wǒ rènshi de lǎoshī. — the teacher (whom) I know.  
    Note: 的 links the modifying clause to the noun.
    

---

## 10) Comparisons

- 比 (bǐ) — A 比 B + adj. (A is more … than B)  
    他比我高。Tā bǐ wǒ gāo. — He is taller than me.
    
- 没有 (méiyǒu) — A 没有 B + adj. (A is not as … as B)  
    我没有你高。Wǒ méiyǒu nǐ gāo.
    

---

## 11) Common connectors & complex sentences

- 因为…所以… (yīnwèi… suǒyǐ…) — because... therefore...
    
- 如果…就… (rúguǒ… jiù…) — if... then...
    
- 虽然…但是… (suīrán… dànshì…) — although... but...
    
- 除了…还/也… — besides... also...
    

---

## 12) Passive: 被 (bèi)

- Subject + 被 + agent + verb + (了)  
    他被雨淋湿了。Tā bèi yǔ lín shī le. — He got wet by the rain.
    
- Use carefully; often Chinese prefers active constructions.
    

---

## 13) Tone tips & common pronunciation traps

- **Tone sandhi:** 不 (bù) changes to second tone (bú) before another fourth-tone syllable; 一 (yī) changes depending on following tone.
    
- **Neutral tone (轻声):** Many function words and particles are neutral.
    
- **Practice:** Drill minimal pairs and tones with audio. Watch mouth shape for j/q/x and zh/ch/sh vs z/c/s.

## 14) Practical mini-lessons (examples + translations)

### Lesson A — Introductions

1. 你好，我叫王明。Nǐ hǎo, wǒ jiào Wáng Míng. — Hello, my name is Wang Ming.
    
2. 我是学生。Wǒ shì xuésheng. — I am a student.
    
3. 我来自印度。Wǒ láizì Yìndù. — I come from India.
    

### Lesson B — Ordering at a restaurant

1. 我想点菜。Wǒ xiǎng diǎn cài. — I want to order dishes.
    
2. 请给我一碗米饭。Qǐng gěi wǒ yì wǎn mǐfàn. — Please give me a bowl of rice.
    
3. 买单！Mǎidān! — The bill, please!
    

### Lesson C — Past experiences

1. 我去过上海。Wǒ qù guo Shànghǎi. — I have been to Shanghai.
    
2. 他昨天没来。Tā zuótiān méi lái. — He didn’t come yesterday.

### Exercise 1 — Translate

a) I don’t have time.  
b) Have you eaten?  
c) She is taller than me.  
d) I saw him yesterday.  
e) Where are you going?

### Exercise 2 — Fill measure words

1. 一 ___ 书 (one book)
    
2. 三 ___ 纸 (three sheets of paper)
    
3. 两 ___ 鞋 (two shoes as a pair)
    

### Answers

c) 她比我高。Tā bǐ wǒ gāo.  
d) 我昨天看见他了。Wǒ zuótiān kànjiàn tā le.  
e) 你去哪里？Nǐ qù nǎlǐ?

Ex2:

1. 一本书 (yì běn shū)
    
2. 三张纸 (sān zhāng zhǐ)
    
3. 一双鞋 (yì shuāng xié)

## 16) Common beginner mistakes to avoid

- Ignoring tones — meaning changes with tone.
    
- Using 是 with adjectives (usually unnecessary): don’t say _他是高_; say _他很高_.
    
- Missing measure words: say 一**个**人 not 一人 in spoken Chinese.
    
- Confusing 了 (completed) and 过 (experience) — 了 often signals completed action or change; 过 signals experience at least once.

## 17) 8-week beginner study plan (practical)

Week 1: Pinyin + tones + numbers + pronouns + 50 core vocab.  
Week 2: Basic verbs + SVO sentences + question words + practice daily dialogues.  
Week 3: Measure words + time phrases + present/past negation (不 vs 没).  
Week 4: Particles: 的, 了, 过, 着 + more vocab (100 total).  
Week 5: Complements (resultative & directional) + serial verbs.  
Week 6: 把/被 structures + comparisons + conjunctions.  
Week 7: Read short texts, write simple paragraphs, practice speaking with native audio.  
Week 8: Consolidate, create 50 self-made dialogues, start HSK1 vocabulary & sample tests.

Daily routine (30–60 min): 10 min pronunciation, 10–20 min new vocab & grammar, 10–20 min listening/speaking + 5–10 min review.

## 19) Cheat-sheet (key forms)

- SVO; time/manner before verb.
    
- Negation: 不 (bù) / 没 (méi).
    
- Questions: 吗, 谁, 什么, 哪里, 为什么, 怎么, 什么时候.
    
- Key particles: 的, 了, 过, 着, 把, 被.
    
- Measure words: 个, 本, 张, 只, 双.
    
- Modal verbs: 会, 能, 可以, 要, 想.
    
- Comparisons: 比, 没有.
    
- Connectors: 因为…所以…, 如果…就….
    

---

## 20) Next steps — actionable

1. **Learn pinyin and tones thoroughly** — 1 week.
    
2. **Memorize 150 highest-frequency words** (pronouns, common verbs, numbers, measure words, basic nouns).
    
3. **Make 50 real sentences** about your life and practice them aloud daily.
    
4. **Record yourself** reading these sentences and compare with native audio.
    
5. **Start reading** one simple graded reader or HSK1 passages within 4–6 weeks.
