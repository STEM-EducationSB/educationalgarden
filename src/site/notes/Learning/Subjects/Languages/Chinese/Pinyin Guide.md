## 1. What Pinyin Actually Is (Clear the Confusion First)

**Pinyin is NOT Chinese.**  
It is a **pronunciation system** using the **Latin alphabet** to represent **Mandarin sounds**.

Each Chinese syllable =  
**Initial (consonant) + Final (vowel part) + Tone**

Example:

- **mā** = m (initial) + a (final) + 1st tone
    

Every spoken Chinese word fits into this structure.

---

## 2. The 4 Tones + Neutral Tone (Critical)

Tones are **non-negotiable**. Ignore them and you are speaking a different word.

|Tone|Mark|Pitch|Example|Meaning|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|1st|¯|High & flat|mā|mother|
|2nd|ˊ|Rising|má|hemp|
|3rd|ˇ|Dip (low → rise)|mǎ|horse|
|4th|ˋ|Sharp fall|mà|scold|
|Neutral|none|Light, short|ma|question particle|

**Hard truth:**  
If you cannot hear or reproduce tones, **Chinese will never sound right**.

## 3. Initials (Consonants) – FULL LIST

### A. Simple Initials (English-like)

b, p, m, f  
d, t, n, l  
g, k, h  
j, q, x  
z, c, s  
r

### B. Retroflex (Tongue curls back)

zh, ch, sh

### C. Special

y, w (mostly helpers for vowels)

---

### IMPORTANT Pronunciation Traps

|Letter|Not English|
|---|---|
|b|unaspirated **p**|
|p|strong puff of air|
|d|unaspirated **t**|
|t|strong puff|
|g|unaspirated **k**|
|k|strong puff|
|q|like “**chee**”|
|x|soft “**shee**”|
|zh|like **j** but tongue curled|
|ch|aspirated zh|
|sh|deeper than English sh|
|r|like French **r** + English **j**|

## 4. Finals (Vowel Systems) – FULL LOGIC

### A. Simple Vowels

a, o, e, i, u, ü

|Vowel|Sound|
|---|---|
|a|father|
|o|“aw”|
|e|uh (not ee)|
|i|ee|
|u|oo|
|ü|French **u** / German **ü**|

---

### B. Compound Finals

ai, ei, ao, ou  
an, en, ang, eng, ong  
ia, ie, iao, iu  
ua, uo, uai, ui  
ian, in, iang, ing  
uan, un, uang, ong  
üe, üan, ün

Yes — it looks big.

## 5. The i / u / ü Rule (This Confuses Everyone)

### After **j q x**, **ü is written as u**

|Written|Actual|
|---|---|
|ju|jü|
|qu|qü|
|xu|xü|

This is **mandatory**, not optional.

---

## 6. Special Syllables (zi, ci, si, zhi…)

These **do NOT use normal “i”**.

|Written|Actual Sound|
|---|---|
|zi ci si|buzzing vowel|
|zhi chi shi ri|retroflex buzzing|

Do NOT say “zee” or “shee”.

---

## 7. Tone Sandhi (Tone Changes You MUST Know)

### A. Two 3rd Tones Together

First becomes **2nd tone**.

|Written|Spoken|
|---|---|
|nǐ hǎo|ní hǎo|

### B. 不 (bù)

- Before 4th tone → **bú**
    
- Otherwise → bù
    

### C. 一 (yī)

- Before 4th → **yí**
    
- Before others → **yì**
    
- Alone → yī
    

---

## 8. How Pinyin Is Actually Written

Tone marks go on:

1. a
    
2. o
    
3. e
    
4. i / u / ü (last resort)
    

Example:

- xiǎo (not xiăo)
    
- guó (not gúo)
    

---

## 9. Why Pinyin Is Finite (Good News)

Mandarin has about **400 base syllables**.  
With tones → about **1,300 total sounds**.

That is **less than English**.

Meaning:

> Chinese pronunciation is **closed-system**.  
> Once mastered, nothing new appears.

---

## 10. A1-Level Mastery Checklist

You are A1-solid when you can:

- Hear and reproduce all 4 tones
    
- Read Pinyin without guessing
    
- Distinguish:
    
    - j/q/x vs zh/ch/sh
        
    - z/c/s vs zh/ch/sh
        
- Say ü correctly
    
- Apply tone sandhi naturally
    

If you cannot do these, **do not rush characters**.
