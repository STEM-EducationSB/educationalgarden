# **ITALIAN PRONOUNS — FULL TUTORIAL (A1–C2)**

Italian pronouns fall into categories. We’ll go through:

1. **Subject pronouns**
    
2. **Direct object pronouns**
    
3. **Indirect object pronouns**
    
4. **Reflexive pronouns**
    
5. **Combined (double) pronouns**
    
6. **Stressed / disjunctive pronouns**
    
7. **Ne & Ci**
    
8. **Relative pronouns**
    
9. **Interrogative pronouns**
    
10. **Demonstrative pronouns**
    
11. **Possessive pronouns**
    
12. **Indefinite pronouns**
    
13. **Formal and polite forms**
    
14. **C2-level nuances**
    

Let’s go step by step.

---

# **1. SUBJECT PRONOUNS (Pronomi soggetto)**

Used when the verb needs clarity. Often dropped because Italian conjugation already shows who is acting.

| Italian     | Meaning        | Notes                        |
| ----------- | -------------- | ---------------------------- |
| io          | I              |                              |
| tu          | you (informal) |                              |
| lui / egli  | he             | _egli = very formal/written_ |
| lei / ella  | she            | _ella = very formal/written_ |
| Lei         | you (formal)   | Always capitalised           |
| noi         | we             |                              |
| voi         | you all        |                              |
| loro / essi | they           | _essi = written/formal_      |

**Examples:**

- Io parlo italiano.
    
- Parli inglese? (subject pronoun dropped)
    

---

# **2. DIRECT OBJECT PRONOUNS (Pronomi diretti)**

Used for the person/thing receiving the action directly.

|Italian|Meaning|
|---|---|
|mi|me|
|ti|you|
|lo|him / it (masc)|
|la|her / it (fem)|
|ci|us|
|vi|you all|
|li|them (masc)|
|le|them (fem)|

**Placement:** before the conjugated verb or attached to infinitives, gerunds, imperatives.

**Examples:**

- Lui **mi** vede. (He sees me.)
    
- Voglio **lo** comprare / Voglio **comprarlo**.
    
- **Li** ho visti ieri.
    

---

# **3. INDIRECT OBJECT PRONOUNS (Pronomi indiretti)**

Used when the verb takes **a + person**.

|Italian|Meaning|
|---|---|
|mi|to me|
|ti|to you|
|gli|to him|
|le|to her|
|Le|to you (formal)|
|ci|to us|
|vi|to you all|
|gli|to them|

**Note:** “gli” = **to him** and **to them**.

**Examples:**

- Ti **do** un consiglio.
    
- Gli **parlo** ogni giorno.
    
- Ho scritto **loro** una lettera. (formal written alternative)
    

---

# **4. REFLEXIVE PRONOUNS (Pronomi riflessivi)**

|Italian|Meaning|
|---|---|
|mi|myself|
|ti|yourself|
|si|himself/herself/yourself (formal)|
|ci|ourselves|
|vi|yourselves|
|si|themselves|

Used with reflexive verbs like **alzarsi**, **lavarsi**, **sentirsi**, etc.

**Examples:**

- Mi sveglio alle sette.
    
- Si chiama Marco.
    
- Ci vediamo domani.
    

---

# **5. COMBINED PRONOUNS (Pronomi combinati)**

These combine **indirect + direct pronouns**. They sound scary at first, but they are systematic.

|Indirect + Direct|Combined Form|
|---|---|
|mi + lo/la/li/le|me lo / me la / me li / me le|
|ti + lo/la/li/le|te lo / te la / te li / te le|
|gli/le + lo/la/li/le|glielo / gliela / glieli / gliele|
|ci + lo/la/li/le|ce lo / ce la / ce li / ce le|
|vi + lo/la/li/le|ve lo / ve la / ve li / ve le|
|gli (pl.) + lo/la/li/le|glielo / gliela / glieli / gliele|

**Examples:**

- Te lo do. (I give it to you.)
    
- Glieli ho mandati. (I sent them to him/her/them.)
    
- Ce la fai? (Can you do it?)
    

---

# **6. STRESSED / DISJUNCTIVE PRONOUNS (Pronomi tonici)**

Used for emphasis, after prepositions (di, a, con, per), and in some special structures.

|Italian|Meaning|
|---|---|
|me|me|
|te|you|
|lui|him|
|lei|her|
|sé|oneself|
|noi|us|
|voi|you all|
|loro|them|

**Examples:**

- Parlo **con te**.
    
- È per **lui**.
    
- Lo farò **io**, non tu.
    
- Pensa solo a **sé**.
    

---

# **7. CI & NE – HIGH-IMPORTANCE PRONOUNS**

### **CI** – four uses

1. **“we/us”**
    
2. **replaces a phrase with “a + place”**
    
    - Vado a Roma. → **Ci** vado.
        
3. **replaces “a + thing/person”**
    
    - Pensi al problema? → **Ci** penso.
        
4. **forms pronominal verbs**
    
    - ricordarsi → ricordar**ci**
        

### **NE** – two huge uses

1. **replaces “di + something/someone”**
    
    - Hai paura dei cani? → No, **non ne ho**.
        
2. **express quantities**
    
    - Quanti libri hai? → **Ne ho** tre.
        
    - Vorrei delle mele. → **Ne vorrei** due.
        

---

# **8. RELATIVE PRONOUNS (Pronomi relativi)**

|Pronoun|Meaning|Use|
|---|---|---|
|che|who/that/which|Universal, no gender/number change|
|cui|which/whom|After prepositions|
|il quale / la quale / i quali / le quali|who/which|Formal, agrees in gender/number|
|ciò che|that which|Abstract ideas|
|quanto|as much as|Literary|

**Examples:**

- La ragazza **che** conosco.
    
- Il libro **di cui** ti ho parlato.
    
- La persona **con la quale** lavoro.
    

---

# **9. INTERROGATIVE PRONOUNS**

|Italian|Meaning|
|---|---|
|chi|who|
|che cosa / cosa / che|what|
|quale / quali|which|
|quanto/a/i/e|how much / how many|

**Examples:**

- **Chi** è?
    
- **Cosa** vuoi?
    
- **Quale** preferisci?
    

---

# **10. DEMONSTRATIVE PRONOUNS (THIS/THAT)**

|Italian|Meaning|
|---|---|
|questo/a/i/e|this/these|
|quello/a/i/e|that/those|

**Examples:**

- **Questo** è migliore.
    
- Preferisco **quello**.
    

---

# **11. POSSESSIVE PRONOUNS (mine/yours/etc.)**

|Italian|Masculine Sing.|Feminine Sing.|Masc. Plural|Fem. Plural|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|mine|il mio|la mia|i miei|le mie|
|yours (tu)|il tuo|la tua|i tuoi|le tue|
|yours (Lei)|il Suo|la Sua|i Suoi|le Sue|
|his/her|il suo|la sua|i suoi|le sue|
|ours|il nostro|la nostra|i nostri|le nostre|
|yours (voi)|il vostro|la vostra|i vostri|le vostre|
|theirs|il loro|la loro|i loro|le loro|

**Examples:**

- Il mio è migliore.
    
- La tua è pronta?

# **12. INDEFINITE PRONOUNS**

Used for general, unspecified things.

|Italian|Meaning|
|---|---|
|qualcuno|someone|
|qualcosa|something|
|ognuno/ciascuno|each one|
|nessuno|no one|
|niente/nulla|nothing|
|chiunque|whoever|
|qualunque/qualsiasi|whichever|
|tutto|everything|

---

# **13. FORMAL AND POLITE FORMS**

- **Lei** = you formal (subject)
    
- **Le** = to you formal (indirect)
    
- **La** = you formal (direct)
    
- Always capitalised.
    

**Examples:**

- **Lei** come si chiama?
    
- La ringrazio. (I thank you.)
    
- Le scrivo domani. (I will write to you.)
    

---

# **14. C2-LEVEL NUANCES**

Here’s where Italian pronouns get advanced:

### **1. “Ne” with abstract reference**

- Ne sono convinto.  
    (I am convinced of it.)
    

### **2. “Ci” to express involvement**

- Non **ci** credo. (I don’t believe it.)
    
- Non **ci** sento. (I can’t hear.)
    

### **3. Redundant pronouns (Italian doubles pronouns for clarity)**

- A Giulia **le** ho parlato già.
    
- A me **mi** piace. (informal but common)
    

### **4. “Che” replacing “il quale” in speech**

- L’uomo **che** ho visto.  
    (C2 includes knowing _when not_ to use “il quale”.)
    

### **5. “Lo” as a neuter pronoun**

Used for ideas, adjectives, clauses.

- **Lo** so. (I know it.)
    
- **Lo** voglio. (I want that situation.)
    
- È difficile, **lo** capisco.
    

### **6. Ellipsis**

In high-level writing, pronouns drop when context is absolute.
