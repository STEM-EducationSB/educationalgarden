# **Module 1 – The Basics of Italian**

## **1. The Italian Alphabet**

Italian uses the **Latin alphabet** with 21 native letters.

**Native Italian letters:**  
A B C D E F G H I L M N O P Q R S T U V Z  
Missing: J, K, W, X, Y (used only in foreign words)

## **2. Pronunciation (Simplified)**

- **A** – “ah”
    
- **E** – “eh” (open), sometimes “ey”
    
- **I** – “ee”
    
- **O** – “oh”
    
- **U** – “oo”
    
- **C** + a/o/u = “k”
    
- **C** + e/i = “ch” (as in _cheese_)
    
- **G** + a/o/u = hard “g” (go)
    
- **G** + e/i = soft “j” (juice)
    

## **3. Basic Greetings**

|Italian|Pronunciation|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|Ciao|chow|Hi / Bye|
|Buongiorno|bwohn-jor-no|Good morning|
|Buonasera|bwo-na-se-ra|Good evening|
|Buonanotte|bwo-na-not-te|Good night|
|Arrivederci|ah-ree-veh-der-chee|Goodbye|
|Come stai?|koh-meh sty|How are you? (informal)|
|Sto bene|sto beh-neh|I am fine|

# **Module 2 – Articles**

Italian has **gender** (masculine/feminine) and **number** (singular/plural).

## **1. Definite Articles (THE)**

|Gender|Singular|Plural|
|---|---|---|
|Masculine|il / lo / l’|i / gli|
|Feminine|la / l’|le|

Examples:

- **il** libro = the book
    
- **lo** studente = the student
    
- **l’** amico = the friend
    
- **i** libri = the books
    
- **gli** studenti = the students
    
- **le** ragazze = the girls
    

## **2. Indefinite Articles (A/AN)**

|Gender|Singular|
|---|---|
|Masculine|un / uno|
|Feminine|una / un’|

Examples:

- un ragazzo = a boy
    
- una ragazza = a girl
    
- un amico = a friend
    
- uno studente = a student
    

---

# **Module 3 – Nouns & Gender**

- Words ending in **-o** → usually masculine
    
- Words ending in **-a** → usually feminine
    
- Words ending in **-e** → can be either (learn individually)
    

**Plural Rules:**

- **-o → -i**
    
- **-a → -e**
    
- **-e → -i**
    

Examples:

- ragazzo → ragazzi
    
- ragazza → ragazze
    
- cane → cani

# **Module 4 – Essential Verbs**

Let’s start with the two “king verbs.”

## **1. Essere (to be)**

|Italian|Pronunciation|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|io sono|ee-oh soh-no|I am|
|tu sei|too say|you are|
|lui/lei è|loo-ee/lay eh|he/she is|
|noi siamo|noy syah-mo|we are|
|voi siete|voy syay-te|you all are|
|loro sono|lo-ro soh-no|they are|

**Examples:**

- Io sono indiano = I am Indian
    
- Lei è bella = She is beautiful
    

## **2. Avere (to have)**

|Italian|Pronunciation|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|io ho|ee-oh oh|I have|
|tu hai|too eye|you have|
|lui/lei ha|ha|he/she has|
|noi abbiamo|ab-byah-mo|we have|
|voi avete|ah-veh-te|you all have|
|loro hanno|an-no|they have|

**Examples:**

- Ho un gatto = I have a cat
    
- Abbiamo tempo = We have time

# **Module 5 – Regular Verb Conjugation (Present Tense)**

Italian has **three verb groups**:

- **-are verbs** (mangiare = to eat)
    
- **-ere verbs** (prendere = to take)
    
- **-ire verbs** (dormire = to sleep)
    

## **1. -ARE**

|Subject|Mangiare (to eat)|
|---|---|
|io|mangio|
|tu|mangi|
|lui/lei|mangia|
|noi|mangiamo|
|voi|mangiate|
|loro|mangiano|

## **2. -ERE**

|Subject|Prendere|
|---|---|
|io|prendo|
|tu|prendi|
|lui/lei|prende|
|noi|prendiamo|
|voi|prendete|
|loro|prendono|

## **3. -IRE**

|Subject|Dormire|
|---|---|
|io|dormo|
|tu|dormi|
|lui/lei|dorme|
|noi|dormiamo|
|voi|dormite|
|loro|dormono|

# **Module 6 – Basic Sentence Structure**

Italian follows **Subject – Verb – Object**, like English.

Examples:

- Io mangio una mela = I eat an apple
    
- Lei vede il cane = She sees the dog
    
- Noi andiamo a scuola = We go to school
    

**Pronouns can be dropped** because the verb ending shows the subject.

“Mangio” alone means **I eat**.

---

# **Module 7 – Negation**

Negation is very easy. Use **non** before the verb.

Examples:

- Non capisco = I do not understand
    
- Non voglio = I don’t want
    
- Non ho soldi = I don’t have money

# **Module 9 – Asking Questions**

Use:

1. **Come?** = How?
    
2. **Cosa?** = What?
    
3. **Perché?** = Why?
    
4. **Chi?** = Who?
    
5. **Dove?** = Where?
    

**Question examples:**

- Dove vai? = Where are you going?
    
- Perché studi italiano? = Why are you studying Italian?
    
- Cosa fai? = What are you doing?
