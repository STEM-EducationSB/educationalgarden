# German Functional Words

### 1. Conditional Words

Used to express conditions, hypotheses, or alternatives.

- wenn – /vɛn/ – if
    
- falls – /falz/ – in case
    
- sofern – /zoˈfɛʁn/ – provided that
    
- vorausgesetzt, dass – /fɔʁˈaʊ̯sɡəzɛtst das/ – assuming that
    
- nur wenn – /nuː vɛn/ – only if
    
- andernfalls – /ˈandɐnˌfals/ – otherwise
    
- es sei denn – /ɛs zaɪ̯ dɛn/ – unless
    
- damit nicht – /ˈdaːmɪt nɪçt/ – lest
    

---

### 2. Logical / Control Flow Words

Used for sequencing, consequence, contrast, and reasoning.

- dann – /dan/ – then
    
- deshalb – /ˈdɛsˌhalp/ – therefore
    
- somit – /ˈzoːmɪt/ – thus
    
- folglich – /ˈfɔlɡlɪç/ – hence
    
- also – /ˈalzo/ – so
    
- allerdings – /ˈalɐdɪŋs/ – however
    
- dennoch – /ˈdɛnɔx/ – nevertheless
    
- stattdessen – /ˈʃtatˌdɛsn̩/ – instead
    
- währenddessen – /ˈvɛːʁəntˌdɛsn̩/ – meanwhile
    
- infolgedessen – /ˈɪnˌfɔlɡəˌdɛsn̩/ – consequently
    
- dementsprechend – /ˈdeːmɛntˌʃpʁɛçn̩t/ – accordingly

### 4. Relative & Demonstrative Words

**a) Demonstratives**

- dies – /diːs/ – this
    
- das – /das/ – that
    
- diese – /ˈdiːzə/ – these
    
- jene – /ˈjeːnə/ – those
    

**b) Relative Pronouns**

- der – /deːɐ̯/ – who / that (m.)
    
- die – /diː/ – who / that (f./pl.)
    
- das – /das/ – which / that (n.)
    
- dessen – /ˈdɛsn̩/ – whose (m./n.)
    
- deren – /ˈdeːʁən/ – whose (f./pl.)
    
- was – /vas/ – what (free relative)

- wann – /van/ – when

- wie – /viː/ – how

- wann auch immer – /van aʊx ˈɪmɐ/ – whenever
