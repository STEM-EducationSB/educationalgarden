## 1. First-Person Pronouns (I / We)

### 1.1 “I” (저 vs 나)

|Korean|Meaning|Politeness|Pronunciation|
|---|---|---|---|
|저|I (humble)|Polite|_jeo_ /tɕʌ/|
|나|I|Casual|_na_ /na/|

**Examples:**

- 저는 학생이에요  
    _jeo-neun hak-saeng-i-e-yo_  
    “I am a student” (polite)
    
- 나는 집에 가  
    _na-neun jip-e ga_  
    “I’m going home” (casual)
    

---

### 1.2 “We” (우리 vs 저희)

|Korean|Meaning|Politeness|Pronunciation|
|---|---|---|---|
|우리|we / our|Neutral|_u-ri_ /uɾi/|
|저희|we (humble)|Polite|_jeo-hi_ /tɕʌhi/|

**Important cultural note:**  
**우리** is often used to mean **“my”**.

- 우리 엄마 → _my_ mother
    
- 우리 집 → _my_ house
    

---

## 2. Second-Person Pronouns (You) ⚠️

**Blunt truth:** Using “you” directly in Korean is often rude.

### 2.1 Common Forms (Use Carefully)

|Korean|Meaning|Use|Pronunciation|
|---|---|---|---|
|너|you|Very casual|_neo_ /nʌ/|
|당신|you|Formal / confrontational|_dang-sin_ /taŋsin/|

**Examples:**

- 너 뭐 해?  
    _neo mwo hae_  
    “What are you doing?”
    

⚠️ **당신** is often used in:

- Arguments
    
- Songs
    
- Married couples (wife → husband)
    

---

### 2.2 What Koreans Prefer Instead

They usually **avoid “you”** and use:

- Names
    
- Titles
    
- Zero pronoun (just omit it)
    

Examples:

- 민수 씨 뭐 해요?  
    “Minsu, what are you doing?”
    
- 지금 가요?  
    “Are (you) going now?”
    

---

## 3. Third-Person Pronouns (He / She / They)

### 3.1 Singular

|Korean|Meaning|Pronunciation|
|---|---|---|
|그|he / that person|_geu_ /kɯ/|
|그녀|she|_geu-nyeo_ /kɯɲʌ/|

⚠️ **그녀** is mostly **written Korean**.  
In speech, people say:

- 그 사람 → that person
    
- 걔 → that kid (casual)
    

---

### 3.2 Plural

Add **들** for plural emphasis.

|Korean|Meaning|Pronunciation|
|---|---|---|
|그들|they|_geu-deul_|
|그녀들|they (female)|_geu-nyeo-deul_|

Again, rarely spoken.

---

## 4. Demonstrative Pronouns (This / That)

|Korean|Meaning|Pronunciation|
|---|---|---|
|이거|this (thing)|_i-geo_|
|그거|that (near listener)|_geu-geo_|
|저거|that (far from both)|_jeo-geo_|

**Example:**

- 이거 뭐예요?  
    _i-geo mwo-ye-yo_  
    “What is this?”
    

---

## 5. Reflexive Pronoun (Self)

|Korean|Meaning|Pronunciation|
|---|---|---|
|자기|self|_ja-gi_|
|자신|oneself (formal)|_ja-sin_|

**Examples:**

- 자기를 믿어요  
    “Believe in yourself”
    

---

## 6. Possessive Forms (My / Your / His)

Just add **의 (-ui)**, often dropped in speech.

|Korean|Meaning|Pronunciation|
|---|---|---|
|내|my|_nae_|
|네|your|_ne_ (sounds like _nae_)|
|그의|his|_geu-ui_|

**Common spoken forms:**

- 내 책 → my book
    
- 네 이름 → your name

## 8. Final Reality Check

If you speak Korean like English and say:

> “You, you, you…”

You will sound unnatural.

**Correct Korean habit:**

- Drop pronouns
    
- Use names/titles
    
- Let context do the work
