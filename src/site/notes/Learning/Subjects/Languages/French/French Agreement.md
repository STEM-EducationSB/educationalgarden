# **FRENCH AGREEMENT RULES – FULL TUTORIAL**

French past participle agreement depends mostly on:

1. The auxiliary verb (**avoir** or **être**)
    
2. Whether the **direct object** comes _before_ the verb
    
3. Whether the verb is **reflexive**
    
4. Whether the verb is **irregular**
    
5. Whether the tense is **compound** (passé composé, plus-que-parfait, passé antérieur, etc.)
    

Let’s break every rule down.

---

# **1. Agreement of Past Participle with “être”**

With **être**, the past participle ALWAYS agrees with the subject.

### Formula:

**Subject + être + past participle (agreeing)**

### Examples:

- **Elle est allée.**
    
- **Ils sont partis.**
    
- **Marie et Sophie sont arrivées.**
    

Agreement:

- Feminine singular → +e
    
- Masculine plural → +s
    
- Feminine plural → +es
    

### Used with:

- Most motion verbs (aller, venir, entrer, sortir, monter, descendre, etc.)
    
- All **reflexive verbs**
    

---

# **2. Agreement of Past Participle with “avoir”**

With **avoir**, the past participle NEVER agrees with the subject.

BUT…

It **agrees with the direct object IF the direct object comes before the verb.**

### **Rule:**

If the direct object is **before the verb**, the past participle agrees with that object.

### Examples:

### A. Object **after** the verb → NO agreement

- **J’ai mangé la pomme.**
    
- **Ils ont vu les filles.** (no agreement)
    

### B. Object **before** the verb → AGREEMENT

- **La pomme que j’ai mangée.**  
    (pomme = feminine singular → mangée)
    
- **Les filles que j’ai vues.**  
    (filles = feminine plural → vues)
    

### C. Direct object pronouns also trigger agreement:

- **Je les ai vus.** (if “les” = masculine plural people)
    
- **Je les ai vues.** (if “les” = feminine plural people)
    
- **Je l’ai vue.** (if l’ = feminine)
    

Important: **Indirect objects NEVER trigger agreement**.

- **Je leur ai parlé.**  
    (leur = indirect → no agreement)
    

---

# **3. Agreement with Reflexive Verbs**

Reflexive verbs ALWAYS use **être**, but they DO NOT always agree.

Agreement depends on whether the reflexive pronoun is a **direct** or **indirect** object.

### **Rule:**

1. If the reflexive pronoun is **direct object**, the participle agrees.
    
2. If it is **indirect object**, NO agreement.
    

### Examples:

### A. Reflexive pronoun = direct object → AGREEMENT

- **Elle s’est lavée.**  
    (“she washed herself”: direct object = herself)
    

### B. Reflexive pronoun = indirect object → NO agreement

Look for a body part after the verb:

- **Elle s’est lavé les mains.**  
    (“she washed HER hands”)  
    → direct object = “les mains”, after the verb → no agreement.
    

### More examples:

- **Ils se sont vus.**  
    They saw each other → direct object before verb → agreement.
    
- **Ils se sont parlé.**  
    They spoke to each other → “parler à quelqu’un” (indirect) → no agreement.
    

---

# **4. Agreement with Irregular Verbs**

Irregular verbs follow the **same rules** as all verbs:

- Agreement with **être** → always
    
- Agreement with **avoir** → only with direct object before
    
- Reflexive → depends on object type
    

Only the **form** of the past participle is irregular, not the agreement rule.

Examples:

- **Elle est venue.**
    
- **Les lettres que j’ai écrites.**
    
- **Les photos qu’ils ont prises.**
    
- **Elle s’est mise en route.**
    

Irregular past participles you must know:

- fait
    
- pris
    
- écrit
    
- mis
    
- ouvert
    
- dit
    
- vu
    
- été
    
- eu
    
- bu
    
- connu
    
- voulu
    
- pu
    
- su  
    (and more)
    

But again — their **agreement** behaves normally.

---

# **5. Agreement in All Compound Tenses**

These rules apply to ALL compound tenses, not just **passé composé**.

That includes:

- plus-que-parfait
    
- futur antérieur
    
- conditionnel passé
    
- passé antérieur
    
- passé surcomposé
    
- any compound tense built with **avoir/être**
    

### Example (plus-que-parfait with avoir):

- **La voiture que j’avais vue.**
    

### Example (conditionnel passé with être):

- **Elles seraient arrivées plus tôt.**
    

### Example (futur antérieur with reflexive):

- **Elles se seront maquillées.**
    

Everything stays consistent.

---

# **FULL DECISION TREE (Super Simple to Use)**

1. **Which auxiliary is used?**
    
    - If **être** → agree with subject
        
    - If **avoir** → go to step 2
        
    - If reflexive → go to step 3
        
2. **For avoir verbs:**
    
    - Is there a **direct object before the verb**?
        
        - Yes → agree
            
        - No → no agreement
            
3. **For reflexive verbs:**
    
    - Reflexive pronoun = direct object?
        
        - Yes → agree
            
    - Reflexive pronoun = indirect object OR object after verb?
        
        - No agreement

# **High-Value Quick Examples to Master It Fast**

### **être**

- Il est parti.
    
- Elle est partie.
    
- Elles sont parties.
    

### **avoir**

- J’ai acheté des fleurs.
    
- Les fleurs que j’ai achetées.
    

### **Reflexive**

- Elle s’est réveillée.
    
- Elle s’est brossé les dents. (no agreement)
    

### **Irregular**

- La lettre que j’ai écrite.
    
- Elles sont venues.
    
- Les photos qu’il a prises.
    

### **Compound tenses**

- Ils auraient fini leur travail.
    
- Les chansons qu’il avait chantées.
