## 1. Three Core Ways to Ask Questions in French

French has **three structurally different question forms**. They are not interchangeable in tone.

---

## 2. Intonation (Informal / Spoken)

### Rule

Use **statement word order**, raise your voice at the end.

### Structure

> **Subject + Verb + … ?**

### Examples

- **Tu parles français ?**  
    You speak French?
    
- **Il vient demain ?**  
    He is coming tomorrow?
    

### Use case

- Casual speech
    
- Conversations, friends, family
    
- Most common in daily life
    

---

## 3. “Est-ce que” Construction (Neutral / Safe)

### Rule

Add **est-ce que** at the beginning. Word order stays normal.

### Structure

> **Est-ce que + Subject + Verb + … ?**

### Examples

- **Est-ce que tu parles français ?**
    
- **Est-ce qu’il vient demain ?**
    

### Notes

- _Est-ce que_ literally means “is it that”
    
- Becomes **est-ce qu’** before a vowel
    

### Use case

- Spoken and written
    
- Very learner-friendly
    
- Polite but not formal
    

---

## 4. Inversion (Formal / Written / Careful Speech)

### Rule

**Invert verb and subject**, connect with a hyphen.

### Structure

> **Verb-Subject-… ?**

### Examples

- **Parles-tu français ?**
    
- **Vient-il demain ?**
    

### Important details

- Add **-t-** for pronunciation when needed:
    
    - **A-t-il fini ?**
        
    - **Va-t-elle venir ?**
        

### Use case

- Formal writing
    
- Speeches, literature
    
- News, exams
    

---

## 5. Questions with Question Words

### Core French Question Words

|English|French|
|---|---|
|Who|Qui|
|What|Quoi / Que|
|Where|Où|
|When|Quand|
|Why|Pourquoi|
|How|Comment|
|How much|Combien|
|Which|Quel(le)(s)|

---

## 6. Placement of Question Words

### A. Informal (Question word at the end)

> **Statement + question word ?**

- **Tu vas où ?**
    
- **Il vient quand ?**
    

Very common in speech.

---

### B. Neutral (Est-ce que)

> **Question word + est-ce que + sentence ?**

- **Où est-ce que tu vas ?**
    
- **Pourquoi est-ce qu’il part ?**
    

---

### C. Formal (Inversion)

> **Question word + verb-subject ?**

- **Où vas-tu ?**
    
- **Pourquoi part-il ?**
    

---

## 7. “Qu’est-ce que” vs “Que”

### Informal / Neutral

- **Qu’est-ce que tu fais ?**  
    What are you doing?
    

### Formal

- **Que fais-tu ?**
    

Rule:

- Use **qu’est-ce que** when _que_ is the object and you want clarity.
    
- Use **que** alone mainly in formal inversion.
    

---

## 8. “Qui” as Subject vs Object

### Subject

- **Qui vient ?**  
    (Who is coming?)
    

### Object

- **Qui est-ce que tu vois ?**  
    (Who do you see?)
    

Formal:

- **Qui vois-tu ?**
    

---

## 9. Negative Questions

Negation wraps the verb as usual.

- **Tu ne viens pas ?**
    
- **Est-ce que tu ne comprends pas ?**
    
- **Ne comprends-tu pas ?**
    

Used often to express surprise or expectation.

---

## 10. Tag Questions (“right?”, “isn’t it?”)

French uses:

- **n’est-ce pas ?**
    

Example:

- **Tu viens demain, n’est-ce pas ?**
    

Neutral to formal.

---

## 11. Quick Comparison Table

|Style|Structure|Use|
|---|---|---|
|Intonation|Statement + ?|Casual|
|Est-ce que|Est-ce que + sentence|Safe, neutral|
|Inversion|Verb-subject|Formal|

---

## Bottom Line (Tell-it-like-it-is)

- **Speak casually → intonation**
    
- **Want correctness everywhere → est-ce que**
    
- **Writing / exams → inversion**
    
- Native speakers mix styles constantly
    
- Master _est-ce que_ first, then inversion
