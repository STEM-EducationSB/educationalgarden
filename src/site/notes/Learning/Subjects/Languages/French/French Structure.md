# **FRENCH SENTENCE STRUCTURE – FULL TUTORIAL**

## **1. Basic French Sentence Order (S – V – O)**

French word order is similar to English:  
**Subject + Verb + Object**

Examples:

- **Je mange une pomme.** = I eat an apple.
    
- **Il regarde la télévision.** = He watches television.
    
- **Nous visitons Paris.** = We are visiting Paris.
    

Notes:

- French is stricter than English. You cannot move words freely for style.
    
- Pronouns always come **before** the verb.
    

---

# **2. Negation (ne…pas + variations)**

Negation surrounds the verb:

### **Basic form: ne…pas**

- **Je ne mange pas.** = I am not eating.
    
- **Elle ne comprend pas.** = She doesn’t understand.
    

### **Common variations**

1. **ne…plus** = no longer / not anymore
    
    - Je ne fume plus. = I no longer smoke.
        
2. **ne…jamais** = never
    
    - Il ne vient jamais. = He never comes.
        
3. **ne…rien** = nothing / not anything
    
    - Je ne vois rien. = I see nothing.
        
4. **ne…personne** = nobody
    
    - Je ne connais personne. = I know nobody.
        
5. **ne…que** = only (NOT real negation)
    
    - Je ne mange que du riz. = I only eat rice.
        

Speaking note: in casual speech, **ne** drops:

- Je mange pas. (spoken)
    

---

# **3. Inversion in Questions (Verb–Subject)**

Used in formal writing or formal speech.

Formula:  
**Verb – Subject pronoun + Object?**

Examples:

- **Parles-tu français ?** = Do you speak French?
    
- **Aime-t-il le chocolat ?**  
    (Note the **-t-** added to avoid vowel clash.)
    

Rules:

- Only invert with subject **pronouns**.
    
- With nouns, keep the noun and add a pronoun:
    
    - Marie parle-t-elle anglais ?
        

---

# **4. _Est-ce que_ Questions**

Most common and easiest question form.

Formula:  
**Est-ce que + statement?**

Examples:

- **Est-ce que tu manges ?** = Are you eating?
    
- **Est-ce que vous comprenez ?** = Do you understand?
    

No tense change. No inversion. Very beginner-friendly.

---

# **5. Yes/No Questions vs WH- Questions**

### **Yes/No Questions**

Three ways:

1. **Statement + ?**
    
    - Tu viens ?
        
2. **Est-ce que + statement**
    
    - Est-ce que tu viens ?
        
3. **Inversion**
    
    - Viens-tu ?
        

### **WH- Questions (qui, que, quoi, où, quand, pourquoi, comment, combien)**

Forms:

1. **WH-word + est-ce que + statement**
    
    - **Où est-ce que tu habites ?**
        
2. **WH-word + inversion**
    
    - **Où habites-tu ?**
        
3. **WH-word + statement + ?**
    
    - **Tu habites où ?** (informal but common)
        

---

# **6. Emphasis Structures (c’est… que / qui…)**

Used to highlight a specific element.

### **c’est… que** → Emphasizes everything except the subject

- **C’est le chocolat que j’adore.**  
    = It’s chocolate that I love.
    

### **c’est… qui** → Emphasizes the subject

- **C’est Marie qui chante.**  
    = It’s Marie who is singing.
    

Purpose: Focus attention.

---

# **7. Relative Clauses (qui, que, où, dont, lequel, etc.)**

These connect two clauses.

### **qui** = subject

- La fille **qui parle** est ma sœur.  
    = The girl **who is speaking** is my sister.
    

### **que / qu’** = direct object

- Le livre **que j’ai lu**.  
    = The book **that I read**.
    

### **où** = place or time

- Le restaurant **où nous allons**.  
    = The restaurant **where we are going**.
    

### **dont** = “whose / of which / about which”

- La femme **dont je parle**.  
    = The woman **about whom I am speaking**.
    

---

# **8. Direct/Indirect Speech**

### **Direct speech**

- Il dit : “Je viens.”  
    = He says: “I’m coming.”
    

### **Indirect speech**

No quotation marks + adjust pronouns/tense when necessary.

- Il dit qu’il vient. = He says that he is coming.
    
- Elle demande si je peux l’aider. = She asks if I can help her.
    

Notable: French uses **que**, **si**, **ce que**, **ce qui**, etc. to connect ideas.

---

# **9. Word Order with Pronouns**

Pronoun order is strict in French.

Before the verb:  
**(me/te/se/nous/vous) → (le/la/les) → (lui/leur) → (y) → (en)**

Example:

- **Je le lui donne.** = I give it to him.
    
- **Elle m’en parle.** = She talks to me about it.
    

In negative:  
**Je ne te le donne pas.**

With infinitives:

- Je vais **te le** donner.
    
- Nous voulons **y** aller.
    

---

# **10. Word Order with Adverbs**

### **Simple adverbs (bien, mal, souvent, toujours, déjà):**

Usually placed **after the conjugated verb**:

- Elle parle **bien**.
    
- Il vient **souvent**.
    

### **With compound tenses:**

Place the adverb **between auxiliary and past participle**:

- J’ai **déjà** vu ce film.
    
- Elle a **bien** compris.
    

### **Long adverbs or phrases:**

Placed **after the entire clause**:

- Il parle français **avec confiance**.
    

---

# **11. Avoiding English–French Structure Mistakes**

### **Common traps:**

1. **Do NOT repeat the subject after a pronoun.**  
    Wrong: _Marie elle est ici._  
    Right: **Marie est ici. / Elle est ici.**
    
2. **Do NOT split verb and object with adverbs (English style).**  
    WRONG: Je mange souvent la viande.  
    RIGHT: **Je mange la viande souvent.**  
    or better: **Je mange souvent de la viande.**
    
3. **Do NOT omit pronouns.**  
    French requires them every time.
    
4. **The adjective usually comes AFTER the noun.**
    
    - Une voiture rouge (a red car)
        
5. **Negative word order is fixed.**  
    ne + verb + pas/jamais/etc.
    
6. **France does NOT use “do” in questions.**  
    English: Do you eat?  
    French: **Tu manges ? / Est-ce que tu manges ? / Manges-tu ?**
    
7. **Object pronouns go BEFORE the verb (not after).**
    
    - Je t’aime  
        not: _I love you_ → _J’aime tu_ (WRONG)
