## **1. What is Gender in French?**

Every noun in French is either:

- **Masculine (m)** → usually for male people, some animals, and many things
    
- **Feminine (f)** → usually for female people, some animals, and some things
    

**Examples:**

- Masculine: **un garçon** → a boy, **le livre** → the book
    
- Feminine: **une fille** → a girl, **la maison** → the house
    

---

## **2. Articles / Determiners and Gender**

|Gender|Singular|Plural|English|
|---|---|---|---|
|Masculine|un / le|des / les|a / the / some|
|Feminine|une / la|des / les|a / the / some|

**Examples:**

- **Un étudiant** (male student) → **une étudiante** (female student)
    
- **Le chat** (male cat) → **la chatte** (female cat, if specified)
    

---

## **3. Adjectives and Gender**

Adjectives **change depending on the gender** of the noun:

|Adjective “happy”|Male singular|Female singular|Male plural|Female plural|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|heureux|heureux|heureuse|heureux|heureuses|

**Examples:**

- Il est **heureux** → He is happy
    
- Elle est **heureuse** → She is happy
    
- Ils sont **heureux** → They are happy (male/mixed)
    
- Elles sont **heureuses** → They are happy (female)
    

**Tip:** Usually, to make an adjective feminine, **add -e** at the end. To make it plural, **add -s**.

---

## **4. How to tell the gender of a noun**

1. If it’s a person or animal, often the **biological gender** tells you:
    
    - garçon → masculine, fille → feminine
        
    - chat → male cat, chatte → female cat
        
2. If it’s an object, you have to **learn it**. Some patterns:
    
    - Nouns ending in **-e** → often feminine (la maison, la table)
        
    - Nouns ending in **-age, -ment, -eau, -oir** → usually masculine (le village, le gouvernement, le château)
