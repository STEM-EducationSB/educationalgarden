# **All French Accents (Les accents français)**

French has **5 accents** total. They change pronunciation, meaning, or both.

---

## **1. Accent Aigu ( ´ ) — only on É**

**é** → sounds like “ay” (close to English _say_)

**Examples:**

- **école** (school)
    
- **éléphant** (elephant)
    
- **écouter** (to listen)
    

**Use:** sharp, bright sound.

---

## **2. Accent Grave ( ` ) — on À, È, Ù**

### **è**

Pronounced like “eh” (open), like in _bed_.

- **père** (father)
    
- **très** (very)
    

### **à**

Changes meaning, not sound.

- **a** (has) vs **à** (to)
    

### **ù**

Rare, only in **où** (where) to distinguish from **ou** (or).

---

## **3. Accent Circonflexe (ˆ) — Â, Ê, Î, Ô, Û**

Often indicates an old deleted “s” in the word.  
Pronunciation changes slightly only for **ê** and **ô**.

- **forêt** (forest → old: forest)
    
- **hôpital** (hospital → old: hospital)
    
- **dîner** (dinner)
    

Sometimes lengthens the vowel.

---

## **4. La Cédille ( ¸ ) — only under a C: Ç**

Turns “c” into an **s** sound before _a, o, u_.

- **garçon** (boy)
    
- **leçon** (lesson)
    

Without it, **c** would sound like “k”.

---

## **5. Le Tréma ( ¨ ) — on Ë, Ï, Ü, Ö**

Means the vowel must be **pronounced separately**.

- **naïf** (na-eef)
    
- **Noël** (No-el)
    
- **ambiguë** (am-bi-gyu-eu)
    

---

# **Quick Reference Table**

|Accent|Appearance|Letters used|Pronunciation effect|
|---|---|---|---|
|Aigu|é|only on **é**|“ay”|
|Grave|à, è, ù|a, e, u|open sound on **è**|
|Circonflexe|â, ê, î, ô, û|a, e, i, o, u|length/old “s”|
|Cédille|ç|c before a/o/u|soft “s” sound|
|Tréma|ë, ï, ü, ö|vowel letters|separate pronunciation|
