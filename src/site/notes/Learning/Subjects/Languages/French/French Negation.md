## **1. Standard Negation: `ne…pas`**

This is the basic negation in French, equivalent to “not” in English.

**Structure:**

`Subject + ne + verb + pas + (rest of sentence)`

**Examples:**

- Je **ne parle pas** anglais. → I do **not** speak English.
    
- Il **n’aime pas** les légumes. → He does **not** like vegetables.
    

**Notes:**

- Before a vowel or silent ‘h’, `ne` becomes `n’`:
    
    - Elle **n’**est pas là. → She is not here.
        
- Spoken French often drops `ne`:
    
    - Je **parle pas** anglais.
        

---

## **2. `ne…jamais` (Never)**

Used to express “never.” Replace `pas` with `jamais`.

**Structure:**

`Subject + ne + verb + jamais`

**Examples:**

- Je **ne fume jamais**. → I never smoke.
    
- Ils **n’ouvrent jamais** la fenêtre. → They never open the window.
    

---

## **3. `ne…plus` (No longer / Not anymore)**

Expresses “no longer” or cessation of an action.

**Structure:**

`Subject + ne + verb + plus`

**Examples:**

- Il **ne travaille plus** ici. → He no longer works here.
    
- Je **n’ai plus** de temps. → I have no more time.
    

---

## **4. `ne…personne` (Nobody / No one)**

Used for expressing “nobody” or “no one.” In French, it can act as the subject or object.

**As object:**

`Subject + ne + verb + personne`

- Je **ne vois personne**. → I see nobody.
    

**As subject:**

`Personne + ne + verb`

- **Personne n’est** venu. → Nobody came.
    

---

## **5. `ne…rien` (Nothing / Not…anything)**

Used to express “nothing.”

**Structure:**

`Subject + ne + verb + rien`

**Examples:**

- Je **ne fais rien** aujourd’hui. → I am doing nothing today.
    
- Il **n’a rien dit**. → He said nothing.
    

**Notes:**

- When used as a subject, `Rien` comes before the verb:
    
    - **Rien n’est** impossible. → Nothing is impossible.
        

---

## **6. Double Negation Combinations**

French often combines negatives for emphasis. Common combinations:

|Negative 1|Negative 2|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|ne|pas…jamais|not ever|
|ne|rien…personne|nothing…nobody|
|ne|plus…personne|no one anymore|
|ne|jamais…personne|never anyone|

**Examples:**

- Je **n’ai jamais vu personne** ici. → I have never seen anyone here.
    
- Il **ne mange plus rien**. → He doesn’t eat anything anymore.
    

**Important:** Only one **`ne`** is used; other words combine to complete the negation.

---

## **7. `ne` explétif (Advanced / Literary usage)**

- The **ne explétif** doesn’t make the sentence negative; it’s used after certain verbs, conjunctions, or expressions in formal/literary French.
    
- Mostly after verbs expressing fear, doubt, or prevention.
    

**Structure:**

`Verb + que + ne + subjunctive`

**Examples:**

- Il craint **que tu ne sois en retard**. → He fears that you might be late.
    
- Je doute **qu’il ne vienne**. → I doubt that he will come.
    

**Note:**

- It’s formal; in spoken French, it’s often omitted.
    
- Doesn’t translate literally into English; it’s a stylistic marker.
    

---

### ✅ **Tips for Mastery**

1. Always place the negation around the **conjugated verb**.
    
2. Watch for compound tenses: the `ne` goes **before the auxiliary**, and the second part of the negation (pas/jamais/etc.) **after** it:
    
    - Je **n’ai jamais vu** ce film. → I have never seen this movie.
        
3. Some pronouns may come **between `ne` and the verb**:
    
    - Je **ne le vois jamais**. → I never see him.
        
4. Avoid translating English word-for-word; French negation often has a single negative particle plus an adverb.
