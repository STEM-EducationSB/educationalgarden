## **1. Days, Months, Seasons**

### **Days of the Week**

|French|Pronunciation|English|
|---|---|---|
|lundi|/lœ̃.di/|Monday|
|mardi|/mar.di/|Tuesday|
|mercredi|/mɛʁ.kʁə.di/|Wednesday|
|jeudi|/ʒø.di/|Thursday|
|vendredi|/vɑ̃.dʁə.di/|Friday|
|samedi|/sam.di/|Saturday|
|dimanche|/di.mɑ̃ʃ/|Sunday|

**Notes:**

- Days of the week are **not capitalized** in French.
    
- To say “on Monday,” use `le lundi`:
    
    - Je travaille **le lundi**. → I work on Mondays.

## **2. Telling Time**

### **Basic Structure**

`Il est + hour + heure(s) + minutes`

**Examples:**

- Il est **trois heures**. → It’s three o’clock.
    
- Il est **quatre heures vingt**. → It’s 4:20.
    
- Il est **huit heures et quart**. → It’s 8:15.
    
- Il est **dix heures moins dix**. → It’s 9:50.
    
- Il est **midi / minuit**. → It’s noon / midnight.
    

**Notes:**

- Use **24-hour clock** for schedules: 14h00 → 2 PM.
    
- `et demie` → half past, `et quart` → quarter past, `moins le quart` → quarter to.

## **3. Expressing Duration & Start Points**

### **a) `depuis` (since / for)**

- Used to indicate an action that **started in the past and continues**.
    
- Requires **present tense** for ongoing actions.
    

**Structure:**

`Subject + verb (present) + depuis + time point / duration`

**Examples:**

- Je **travaille ici depuis 2019**. → I have been working here since 2019.
    
- Elle **étudie le français depuis deux ans**. → She has been studying French for two years.
    

---

### **b) `pendant` (for / during)**

- Refers to a **finished action with a duration**.
    

**Examples:**

- J’ai voyagé **pendant deux semaines**. → I traveled for two weeks.
    
- Il a dormi **pendant la réunion**. → He slept during the meeting.
    

---

### **c) `pour` (for – future plan)**

- Used with **future actions** or commitments.
    

**Examples:**

- Je pars **pour deux jours**. → I am leaving for two days.
    
- Elle loue un appartement **pour un an**. → She is renting an apartment for a year.
    

---

## **4. Future vs. Near Future (Futur vs. Futur Proche)**

### **a) Near Future (`futur proche`)**

- Used for actions that are about to happen.
    
- Structure: **aller + infinitive**
    

**Examples:**

- Je **vais manger**. → I am going to eat.
    
- Ils **vont partir demain**. → They are going to leave tomorrow.
    

---

### **b) Simple Future (`futur simple`)**

- Used for actions that will happen further in the future or more formally.
    
- Regular endings: `-ai, -as, -a, -ons, -ez, -ont`
    

**Examples:**

- Je **mangerai** à midi. → I will eat at noon.
    
- Nous **partirons** en vacances cet été. → We will go on vacation this summer.
    

**Tips:**

- Near future often spoken, simple future often written or formal.

## **5. Past Reference Markers**

Certain words/phrases help indicate **when an action happened**, useful with **passé composé** or **imparfait**.

|Marker|Usage|Example|
|---|---|---|
|hier|yesterday|Hier, j’ai vu Marie. → Yesterday, I saw Marie.|
|ce matin / soir|this morning / evening|Ce matin, il a fait froid. → This morning it was cold.|
|la semaine dernière|last week|La semaine dernière, nous sommes allés au cinéma.|
|il y a …|ago|J’ai fini mes devoirs il y a deux heures. → I finished my homework two hours ago.|
|autrefois|in the past / formerly|Autrefois, il vivait à Lyon. → In the past, he lived in Lyon.|
|pendant que|while|Il lisait **pendant que** je dormais. → He was reading while I was sleeping.|

---

### ✅ **Tips for Mastery**

1. Always match **depuis/pendant/pour** with the correct tense: ongoing → `depuis`, finished → `pendant`, planned/future → `pour`.
    
2. For time expressions, remember French **does not capitalize days or months**.
    
3. For telling time, know **et quart / et demie / moins le quart** for easy conversational usage.
    
4. Use **near future** for spoken French; **simple future** for formal or distant events.
    
5. Combine **past markers** with **passé composé** for completed actions, and **imparfait** for ongoing past actions.
