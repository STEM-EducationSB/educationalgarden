# **FRENCH GRAMMAR RULES (Beginner → Intermediate Foundation)**

A complete, streamlined guide.

---

# **1. Nouns (Les noms)**

### **1.1 Gender (Masculine & Feminine)**

Every French noun has a gender.

- **Masculine usually ends with:**
    
    - **-age, -aire, -isme, -ment, -oir, -sme, -é**
        
    - Examples: _le garage, le dictionnaire, le chocolat, le passé_
        
- **Feminine usually ends with:**
    
    - **-tion, -sion, -té, -ette, -ie, -ure**
        
    - Examples: _la nation, la télévision, la beauté, la nourriture_
        

### **1.2 Plurals**

- Add **-s** normally
    
    - _un livre → des livres_
        
- Ends in **-eau, -eu → +x**
    
    - _un bateau → des bateaux_
        
- Ends in **-al → -aux**
    
    - _un animal → des animaux_
        

---

# **2. Articles (Les articles)**

### **2.1 Definite (the)**

- **le** – masculine
    
- **la** – feminine
    
- **l’** – vowel or silent h
    
- **les** – plural
    

### **2.2 Indefinite (a, an, some)**

- **un** – masculine
    
- **une** – feminine
    
- **des** – plural
    

### **2.3 Partitive (some/any)**

- **du** – masculine
    
- **de la** – feminine
    
- **de l’** – vowel
    
- **des** – plural
    

---

# **3. Adjectives (Les adjectifs)**

### **3.1 Agreement**

Adjectives agree in **gender** and **number** with the noun.

- Masculine: _petit_
    
- Feminine: _petite_
    
- Masc. plural: _petits_
    
- Fem. plural: _petites_
    

### **3.2 Position**

Most adjectives come **after** the noun.  
But **BAGS** adjectives come **before** (Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size).

Examples:

- _une belle maison_
    
- _un petit chien_
    

---

# **4. Verbs & Conjugation**

### **4.1 Three regular groups**

1. **-ER** verbs (largest group)
    
    - Example: _parler_ (to speak)
        
    - je parle, tu parles, il parle…
        
2. **-IR** verbs
    
    - Example: _finir_ (to finish)
        
    - je finis, tu finis, il finit…
        
3. **-RE** verbs
    
    - Example: _vendre_ (to sell)
        
    - je vends, tu vends, il vend…
        

### **4.2 Key Irregular Verbs**

You must memorize:

- **être** (to be)
    
- **avoir** (to have)
    
- **aller** (to go)
    
- **faire** (to do/make)**
    
- venir** (to come)
    
- pouvoir (can)
    
- vouloir (want)
    
- devoir (must)
    

These appear in **almost every sentence**.

# **5. Basic Tenses**

### **5.1 Present tense**

Used for current actions.

- _Je mange._ – I eat / I am eating.
    

### **5.2 Passé Composé (Past)**

Structure: **avoir/être + past participle**

- _J’ai mangé._ – I ate.
    

### **5.3 Future proche (Near Future)**

Structure: **aller + infinitive**

- _Je vais manger._ – I am going to eat.
    

### **5.4 Imperfect (Imparfait)**

Used for habits and descriptions.

- _Je mangeais._ – I used to eat / was eating.
    

---

# **6. Negation**

### **Structure**

**ne + verb + pas**

Examples:

- _Je ne mange pas._ – I do not eat.
    
- _Il ne parle pas._ – He does not speak.
    

Before vowels:

- _Je n’aime pas._ – I do not like.
    

---

# **7. Pronouns**

### **7.1 Subject Pronouns**

- je – I
    
- tu – you
    
- il/elle – he/she
    
- nous – we
    
- vous – you (formal/plural)
    
- ils/elles – they
    

### **7.2 Object Pronouns**

- me
    
- te
    
- le/la
    
- nous
    
- vous
    
- les
    
- _Je le vois._ – I see him/it.
    

---

# **8. Question Formation**

### **8.1 With intonation**

Just raise your voice:

- _Tu vas bien ?_
    

### **8.2 With est-ce que**

- _Est-ce que tu comprends ?_
    

### **8.3 Inversion**

- _Comprends-tu ?_
    

---

# **9. Possession**

### **Possessive adjectives:**

- mon, ma, mes – my
    
- ton, ta, tes – your
    
- son, sa, ses – his/her
    
- notre, nos – our
    
- votre, vos – your
    
- leur, leurs – their
    
- _Ma maison_ – my house
    
- _Mes amis_ – my friends
    

---

# **10. Prepositions**

Most common:

- à – to
    
- de – of/from
    
- dans – in
    
- sur – on
    
- sous – under
    
- avec – with
    
- sans – without
    
- pour – for
    
- chez – at someone’s place
    

---

# **11. Agreement Rules You MUST Remember**

1. Past participle agreement with **être**
    
    - _Elle est allée._
        
2. Past participle agreement with **avoir** when direct object is before
    
    - _Les fleurs que j’ai vues._
        
3. Adjectives match the noun in gender/number.
    
4. Verbs match the subject.
    

---

# **12. Useful Sentence Structure Formula**

A strong beginner’s template:

**Subject + verb + article + noun + adjective + preposition + location**

Example:

- _Je mange une pomme rouge dans la cuisine._
    
- I eat a red apple in the kitchen.
