# INTERNATIONAL PHONETIC ALPHABET (IPA)

**Full Tutorial — From Zero to Mastery**

---

## 1. What IPA Actually Is (and What It Is Not)

**IPA = International Phonetic Alphabet**

- A **scientific writing system** for **speech sounds**
    
- Each symbol = **one sound**
    
- Same symbol → same sound → everywhere on Earth
    

### IPA is NOT:

- A language
    
- A script like Devanagari or Arabic
    
- Based on spelling
    

### IPA IS:

- Based on **how sounds are produced**
    
- Used in:
    
    - Linguistics
        
    - Dictionaries
        
    - Accent training
        
    - Speech therapy
        
    - Language documentation
        

**Key principle:**

> _Write what you hear, not what you think is written._

---

## 2. Phones, Phonemes, and Brackets (CRITICAL)

### Phones

- Actual spoken sounds
    
- Written in **square brackets** `[ ]`
    

Example:

- English _pin_ → `[pʰɪn]` (with aspiration)
    

### Phonemes

- Abstract sound units that change meaning
    
- Written in **slashes** `/ /`
    

Example:

- /p/ vs /b/ → _pat_ vs _bat_
    

### Rule

- `/ /` → language system
    
- `[ ]` → real pronunciation

## 3. How Speech Sounds Are Classified

Every sound is defined by **three dimensions**:

### A. Place of Articulation

_Where the sound is made_

### B. Manner of Articulation

_How the airflow is modified_

### C. Voicing

_Whether vocal cords vibrate_

---

## 4. Consonants (FULL)

### 4.1 Places of Articulation

|Place|Description|
|---|---|
|Bilabial|Both lips|
|Labiodental|Lip + teeth|
|Dental|Tongue against teeth|
|Alveolar|Tongue at ridge|
|Postalveolar|Behind alveolar|
|Retroflex|Tongue curled back|
|Palatal|Tongue near hard palate|
|Velar|Tongue near soft palate|
|Uvular|Back of tongue|
|Pharyngeal|Throat|
|Glottal|Vocal cords|

---

### 4.2 Manners of Articulation

|Manner|Description|
|---|---|
|Plosive (Stop)|Complete closure|
|Nasal|Air through nose|
|Fricative|Narrow friction|
|Affricate|Stop + fricative|
|Approximant|Narrow but smooth|
|Lateral|Air around sides|
|Trill|Rapid vibration|
|Tap/Flap|Single quick touch|

---

### 4.3 Voicing

- **Voiced** → vocal cords vibrate
    
- **Voiceless** → no vibration
    

Test: put fingers on throat and speak.

## 6. VOWELS (THIS IS WHERE MOST PEOPLE FAIL)

Vowels are defined by **tongue position**, not lips.

### Dimensions:

1. Height (close → open)
    
2. Backness (front → back)
    
3. Lip rounding
    

---

### 6.1 Cardinal Vowels (Simplified)

|IPA|Example|
|---|---|
|/i/|machine|
|/ɪ/|bit|
|/e/|café|
|/ɛ/|bed|
|/æ/|cat|
|/ɑ/|father|
|/ɔ/|thought|
|/o/|go|
|/ʊ/|book|
|/u/|food|
|/ə/|ago|

---

### The SCHWA /ə/

- Most common vowel in English
    
- Unstressed
    
- Neutral sound
    

Example:

- about → /əˈbaʊt/

## 8. Suprasegmentals (ADVANCED BUT ESSENTIAL)

### Stress

- Primary stress: `ˈ`
    
- Secondary stress: `ˌ`
    

Example:

- development → /dɪˈvɛləpmənt/
    

---

### Length

- Long vowel: `ː`
    

Example:

- sheep → /ʃiːp/
    

---

### Tone (used in Chinese, Thai, etc.)

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|˥|High|
|˧|Mid|
|˩|Low|

Example (Mandarin):

- mā → /ma˥/
    
- mǎ → /ma˨˩˦/
    

---

## 9. Diacritics (Sound Modifiers)

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|ʰ|aspiration|
|̃|nasalized|
|̥|voiceless|
|ː|long|
|̚|unreleased|

Example:

- pin → [pʰɪn]
    
- can’t → [kæ̃nʔ]

Indian languages are **phonetically clean**—IPA fits them perfectly.

## 12. How to ACTUALLY Master IPA

No nonsense path:

1. Memorize **consonant chart**
    
2. Learn **vowel trapezium**
    
3. Transcribe:
    
    - Your own speech
        
    - Dictionary entries
        
4. Compare accents
    
5. Practice minimal pairs:
    
    - /i/ vs /ɪ/
        
    - /p/ vs /b/
        

IPA is skill, not theory.
