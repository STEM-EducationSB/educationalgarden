## **4. Grammar Rules**

1. **Basic Sentence Structure:**  
    `Subject li Verb e Object`
    

- Only **mi / sina** skip `li`.
    
- `e` marks **direct object**.
    
- Example: **mi moku e kili.** → I eat fruit.
    

2. **Modifiers:**
    

- `pi` groups multiple words modifying a noun:  
    **tomo pi jan pona** → house of good person → friend’s house
    

3. **Adverbs / Adjectives:**
    

- Place **after the noun or verb**:  
    **soweli suli** → big animal  
    **mi moku pona** → I eat well / I eat good food
    

4. **Questions:**
    

- Add **ala** for yes/no:  
    **sina sona ala?** → Do you know?
    
- Use **seme** for “what / who / which”:  
    **sina moku seme?** → What are you eating?
    

5. **Imperative:**
    

- Use **o** before command:  
    **o moku!** → Eat!  
    **o tawa!** → Go!
    

6. **Conjunctions:**
    

- **en** → and (for nouns)
    
- **taso** → but / only
    
- **anu** → or

## **5. Example Sentences**

|Toki Pona|English|
|---|---|
|mi moku.|I eat.|
|sina wile moku.|You want to eat.|
|ona li moku e kili.|He eats fruit.|
|mi lukin e suno.|I see the sun.|
|mi tawa tomo sina.|I go to your house.|
|soweli suli li moku e kili.|The big animal eats fruit.|
|mi sona ala e ni.|I don’t know this.|
|jan pona mi li tawa.|My friend is going.|
|o pali!|Do it! / Work!|
|mi pilin e ni: pona!|I feel that this is good!|
