## 1. What Portuguese Is (Big Picture)

- **Language family**: Indo-European → Romance
    
- **Closest relatives**: Spanish, Galician, Italian
    
- **Major standards**:
    
    - **European Portuguese (PT-PT)** – Portugal
        
    - **Brazilian Portuguese (PT-BR)** – Brazil (clearer pronunciation, globally dominant)
        
- **Alphabet**: Latin (26 letters)
    

**Blunt truth**: Learn **Brazilian Portuguese first** unless you specifically need Portugal. More speakers, clearer phonetics, better media coverage.

---

## 2. Alphabet & Core Sounds (Essentials Only)

Portuguese spelling is mostly logical, but **nasal sounds** are the real game-changer.

### Vowels

|Letter|Sound|
|---|---|
|a|ah|
|e|eh / ee|
|i|ee|
|o|oh / oo|
|u|oo|

### Nasal vowels (critical)

- **ã, õ**
    
- **am, an, em, en** at word end
    

Examples:

- **não** = now̃
    
- **pão** = bread
    
- **bem** = well
    

If you ignore nasalization, you will sound foreign immediately.

## 3. Articles (Gender Is Mandatory)

Portuguese nouns are **masculine or feminine**.

|Gender|Singular|Plural|
|---|---|---|
|Masculine|o|os|
|Feminine|a|as|

Examples:

- **o livro** – the book
    
- **a casa** – the house
    

Rule of thumb:

- Ends in **-o** → masculine
    
- Ends in **-a** → feminine  
    (Not perfect, but works 80% of the time.)

## 4. Personal Pronouns (Core Set)

|Person|Pronoun|
|---|---|
|I|eu|
|you (inf.)|você|
|he|ele|
|she|ela|
|we|nós|
|they (m/f)|eles / elas|

**Brazil reality check**:

- **você** replaces **tu** in most situations.
    
- Verb conjugation follows **você = he/she form**.

## 5. Present Tense – The Engine of the Language

### Verb Groups

|Ending|Group|
|---|---|
|-ar|falar (to speak)|
|-er|comer (to eat)|
|-ir|abrir (to open)|

---

### Regular Conjugation Pattern

#### Falar (-ar)

|Pronoun|Form|
|---|---|
|eu|falo|
|você|fala|
|ele/ela|fala|
|nós|falamos|
|eles/elas|falam|

#### Comer (-er)

| eu | como |  
| você | come |  
| nós | comemos |  
| eles | comem |

#### Abrir (-ir)

| eu | abro |  
| você | abre |  
| nós | abrimos |  
| eles | abrem |

**Once you know this pattern, you know hundreds of verbs.**

## 6. Two Verbs You Must Master First

### Ser (permanent)

- identity, origin, profession
    

| eu | sou |  
| você | é |  
| nós | somos |  
| eles | são |

### Estar (temporary)

- state, location, emotion
    

| eu | estou |  
| você | está |  
| nós | estamos |  
| eles | estão |

**Hard rule**:

- _Eu sou estudante_ (I am a student)
    
- _Eu estou cansado_ (I am tired)
    

---

## 7. Negation & Questions

### Negation

- **não + verb**
    

> Eu **não** falo português.

### Questions

- Same sentence, rising intonation
    

> Você fala português?

Simple. No inversion madness like German.

---

## 8. Basic Sentence Structure

**SVO** (Subject–Verb–Object)

> Eu falo português.  
> Ela gosta de música.

Prepositions matter more than word order.

---

## 9. Core Prepositions (High-Frequency)

|Portuguese|Meaning|
|---|---|
|de|of/from|
|em|in/on|
|para|to/for|
|com|with|
|sem|without|

Contractions:

- **de + o = do**
    
- **em + a = na**
    
- **para + o = pro**
    

These are non-optional in real Portuguese.

## 12. Pronunciation Reality Check

Portuguese is **spoken fast**, vowels reduce, and final sounds blur—especially in PT-PT.

**If you read slowly at first, you’re doing it right.**

---

## 13. Strategic Learning Advice (No Sugar-Coating)

- Do **NOT** over-compare with Spanish.
    
- Learn **verbs + prepositions together**.
    
- Shadow Brazilian speech early.
    
- Ignore slang until grammar is stable.
