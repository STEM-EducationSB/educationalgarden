# Russian Grammar — Full Tutorial

Clear, practical, and packed with examples. Every Russian word below has a simple English pronunciation (transliteration) and an IPA transcription so you can read and sound like a native. Read steadily — you’ll build a solid foundation.

---

## Quick guide to the transcription used

- **Transliteration**: simple letters to help English speakers read Russian (e.g., **мама — mama**).
    
- **IPA**: International Phonetic Alphabet for accurate pronunciation (e.g., **мама — [ˈmamə]**).
    
- Stress is not always marked; when important, I’ll show the stressed syllable with an acute accent (́) in the transliteration and in IPA.

# 3. Nouns — gender, number, cases (the heart of Russian)

## 3.1 Gender

Every Russian noun is **masculine**, **feminine**, or **neuter**. Gender affects adjectives, pronouns, and endings.

Typical endings:

- **Masculine**: usually consonant or **-й**.
    
    - **стол — stol — [stol]** (table) — masculine.
        
- **Feminine**: usually **-а, -я**.
    
    - **мама — mama — [ˈmamə]** — feminine.
        
- **Neuter**: usually **-о, -е**.
    
    - **окно — okno — [ɐkˈno]** — _window_.
        

## 3.2 Number

- **Singular** (one): **стол** — _table_.
    
- **Plural** (more than one): **столы — stoly — [ˈstolɨ]** — _tables_.
    

Plural formation varies by gender and ending.

## 3.3 Cases — 6 main cases (what they do)

Russian uses cases (endings change form). Learn these with examples. For each example I show: Russian word (translit — IPA) — English.

### Table of cases with short explanation

1. **Nominative (Имéнительный)** — subject (who/what).
    
    - Example: **Мама идёт. — Mama idyot. — [ˈmamə ɪˈdot]** — _Mom is going._
        
2. **Genitive (Родительный)** — possession / “of” / after some prepositions / amounts.
    
    - Example: **книга мамы — kniga mamy — [ˈknʲigə ˈmamɨ]** — _mom’s book_.
        
3. **Dative (Дательный)** — indirect object — _to/for somebody_.
    
    - Example: **дать маме — dat' mame — [datʲ ˈmamʲe]** — _to give to mom_.
        
4. **Accusative (Винительный)** — direct object / motion to (some cases).
    
    - Example: **вижу книгу — vizhu knigu — [ˈvʲiʐu ˈknʲigu]** — _I see a book_.
        
5. **Instrumental (Творительный)** — _with / by means of_.
    
    - Example: **писать ручкой — pisat' ruchkoy — [pʲɪˈsatʲ ˈrut͡ɕkəj]** — _to write with a pen_.
        
6. **Prepositional (Предложный)** — _about / in / on_ (used after certain prepositions).
    
    - Example: **говорить о маме — govorit' o mame — [gəvɐˈrʲitʲ o ˈmamʲe]** — _to talk about mom_.

## 3.4 Case endings — examples with the noun **мама (mama) — [ˈmamə]** (feminine)

- **Nom.** мама — _mama_ — **мама** — [ˈmamə] — _mom_
    
- **Gen.** мамы — _of mom_ — **мамы** — [ˈmamɨ]
    
- **Dat.** маме — _to mom_ — **маме** — [ˈmamʲe]
    
- **Acc.** маму — _mom (as object)_ — **маму** — [ˈmamʊ]
    
- **Instr.** мамой / мамою — _with mom_ — **мамой** — [ˈmamoj]
    
- **Prep.** о маме — _about mom_ — **о маме** — [o ˈmamʲe]
    

Do the same for **стол (stol) — [stol]** (masculine):

- Nom. **стол — stol — [stol]**
    
- Gen. **стола — stola — [stɐˈla]**
    
- Dat. **столу — stolu — [ˈstolʊ]**
    
- Acc. **стол — stol — [stol]** (same as nominative for inanimate masculine)
    
- Instr. **столом — stolom — [ˈstoləm]**
    
- Prep. **о столе — o stole — [o ˈstolʲe]**
    

And **окно (okno) — [ɐkˈno]** (neuter):

- Nom. **окно — okno — [ɐkˈno]**
    
- Gen. **окна — okna — [ˈoknə]**
    
- Dat. **окну — oknu — [ˈoknʊ]**
    
- Acc. **окно — okno — [ɐkˈno]**
    
- Instr. **окном — oknom — [ˈoknəm]**
    
- Prep. **об окне — ob okne — [ob ˈoknʲe]**
    

**Practice tip:** Memorize endings in small groups (by gender). Use real examples.

# 4. Adjectives — agreement and endings

Adjectives change to match the noun in **gender, number, and case**.

Example adjective **большой — bol'shoy — [bɐlˈʂoj]** (big).

Simple patterns in **Nominative singular**:

- **Masculine**: большой — **большой — bol'shoy — [bɐlˈʂoj]**
    
- **Feminine**: большая — **bol'shaya — [bɐlˈʂajə]**
    
- **Neuter**: большое — **bol'shoye — [bɐlˈʂoje]**
    
- **Plural**: большие — **bol'shiye — [bɐlˈʂijə]**
    

Example sentences:

- **Большой дом — Bol'shoy dom — [bɐlˈʂoj dom]** — _big house_ (masculine).
    
- **Большая книга — Bol'shaya kniga — [bɐlˈʂajə ˈknʲigə]** — _big book_ (feminine).
    

Adjectives decline like nouns — they have cases. E.g., **в большом доме — v bol'shom dome — [v bɐlˈʂom ˈdomʲe]** — _in the big house_ (prep. case).

# 6. Verbs — basics, conjugation, aspect, tenses

## 6.1 Two important ideas: **conjugation** and **aspect**

- **Conjugation**: verb endings change with person and number (I, you, he/she, we, you(pl), they).
    
- **Aspect**: Russian verbs come in **imperfective** (action ongoing/habitual) and **perfective** (action completed / single event). Each basic verb pair looks like: **читать (to read, impf.) — прочитать (to read through, perf.)**.
    

## 6.2 Present tense (only for **imperfective** verbs)

Conjugate **говорить — govorit' — [gəvɐˈrʲitʲ]** (to speak):

- **я говорю — ya govoryu — [ja gɐvɐˈrʲu]** — _I speak_
    
- **ты говоришь — ty govorish' — [tɨ gɐˈvorʲɪʃ]** — _you speak_
    
- **он/она говорит — on/ona govorit — [on/ˈaɡəvɐˈrʲit]** — _he/she speaks_
    
- **мы говорим — my govorim — [mɨ gɐˈvorʲim]** — _we speak_
    
- **вы говорите — vy govorite — [vɨ gɐˈvorʲitʲe]** — _you (pl) speak_
    
- **они говорят — oni govoryat — [ɐˈni gɐˈvorʲat]** — _they speak_
    

(There are two conjugation types; learn by verb endings.)

## 6.3 Past tense

Formed from infinitive stem + **-л** and adjective-like endings for gender/number.  
Verb **читать — chitat' — [t͡ɕɪˈtatʲ]** (to read, impf.) past:

- **я читал — ya chital — [ja t͡ɕɪˈtal]** (male speaker)
    
- **я читала — ya chitala — [ja t͡ɕɪˈtalə]** (female speaker)
    
- **мы читали — my chitali — [mɨ t͡ɕɪˈtalʲi]**
    

## 6.4 Future tense

Two ways:

1. **Perfective verb** — single-word future (perfective).
    
    - **я прочитаю — ya prochitayu — [ja prət͡ɕɪˈtaju]** — _I will read (complete)_
        
2. **Imperfective + auxiliary быть (to be)** — compound future for habitual or ongoing actions.
    
    - **я буду читать — ya budu chitat' — [ja ˈbudu t͡ɕɪˈtatʲ]** — _I will be reading / I will read (habitually)_
        

## 6.5 Imperative (commands)

Formed from verb stem:

- **читать — читай! — chitay! — [t͡ɕɪˈtaj]** — _Read!_ (informal)
    
- **писать — пиши! — pishi! — [pʲiˈʂi]** — _Write!_
    

## 6.6 Reflexive verbs (-ся / -сь)

Add **-ся** to mean _oneself_ or reflexive action.

- **мыться — myt'sya — [ˈmɨt͡sə]** — _to wash oneself_  
    Example: **Я моюсь — Ya moyus' — [ja ˈmojʊsʲ]** — _I am washing myself._
    

## 6.7 Modal verbs (want, can, must)

Common verbs:

- **мочь — moch' — [mot͡ɕ]** — _can_ (present: **я могу — ya mogu — [ja mɐˈgu]**)
    
- **хотеть — khotet' — [xətʲˈetʲ]** — _to want_ (**я хочу — ya khochu — [ja xɐˈt͡ɕu]**)
    

Examples:

- **Я хочу есть. — Ya khochu yest'. — [ja xɐˈt͡ɕu jestʲ]** — _I want to eat._

# 7. Numbers and counting with nouns (important patterns)

Basic numbers:

- **1 — один — odin — [ɐˈdʲin]**
    
- **2 — два — dva — [dva]**
    
- **3 — три — tri — [tri]**
    
- **4 — четыре — chetyre — [t͡ɕɪˈtʲirʲe]**
    
- **5 — пять — pyat' — [pʲatʲ]**
    
- **10 — десять — desyat' — [ˈdʲesʲətʲ]**
    

Counting changes noun cases:

- **1 книга — odin kniga — [ɐˈdʲin ˈknʲigə]** (nominative singular)
    
- **2/3/4 книги — dva/tri/chetyre knigi — [dva ˈknʲigi]** (genitive singular form)
    
- **5+ книг — pyat' knig — [pʲatʲ ˈknʲig]** (genitive plural)
    

Examples:

- **2 книги — Dva knigi — [dva ˈknʲigi]** — _two books_
    
- **7 книг — Sem' knig — [sʲemʲ ˈknʲig]** — _seven books_

# 8. Prepositions and which case they use (common ones)

- **в / во — v / vo — [v] / [vo]** — _in, into_ — **Accusative** (motion into) / **Prepositional** (location).
    
    - **в школу — v shkolu — [v ʂkʊˈlu]** (acc., _into the school_).
        
    - **в школе — v shkole — [v ˈʂkolʲe]** (prep., _in the school_).
        
- **на — na — [na]** — _on / to_ — same rules as **в**.
    
- **с / со — s / so — [s] / [so]** — _with / from_ — **Instrumental** (with) / **Genitive** (from).
    
- **у — u — [u]** — _by/at (someone), possession_ — **Genitive**.
    
    - **у меня — u menya — [u mʲɪˈnʲa]** — _I have / at me_.
        
- **о / об — o / ob — [o] / [ob]** — _about_ — **Prepositional**.
    
- **для — dlya — [dlʲa]** — _for_ — **Genitive**.
    
- **без — bez — [bʲez]** — _without_ — **Genitive**.
    
- **под — pod — [pot]** — _under_ — **Accusative** (motion) / **Instrumental** (location).
    

Remember: preposition → required case rule must be memorized.

# 9. Word order and sentence building

Russian word order is flexible because cases show the role of each word. Typical neutral order is **Subject — Verb — Object** (SVO), like English.

Example:

- **Я читаю книгу. — Ya chitayu knigu. — [ja t͡ɕɪˈtajʊ ˈknʲigu]** — _I read a book._
    

You can move words to emphasize:

- **Книгу читаю я. — Knigu chitayu ya. — [ˈknʲigu t͡ɕɪˈtajʊ ja]** — _It’s the book that I read_ (emphasis on _book_).

# 10. Questions and negatives

### Questions:

- Use question word or rising intonation.
    
- **Кто? — kto? — [kto]** — _Who?_
    
- **Что? — chto? — [ʂto]** — _What?_
    
- **Где? — gde? — [gdʲe]** — _Where?_
    
- **Когда? — kogda? — [kɐˈgda]** — _When?_
    
- **Почему? — pochemu? — [pəˈt͡ɕemu]** — _Why?_
    

Example:

- **Где школа? — Gde shkola? — [gdʲe ʂkɐˈla]** — _Where is the school?_
    

### Negation:

Add **не** before the verb:

- **Я не вижу. — Ya ne vizhu. — [ja nʲe ˈvʲiʐu]** — _I do not see._
    

For nothing/no one: **никто (nobody)**, **ничто (nothing)**, **нигде (nowhere)**.

# 12. Short grammar drills (practice examples with breakdown)

### Example 1 — simple sentence

**Русский:** **Я читаю книгу.** — _Ya chitayu knigu._ — [ja t͡ɕɪˈtajʊ ˈknʲigu]

- **Я (I, nom.)** — subject.
    
- **читаю (read, pres.)** — verb (I form).
    
- **книгу (book, acc.)** — direct object (accusative).
    

**English:** _I am reading a book._

---

### Example 2 — possession

**Русский:** **Это книга мамы.** — _Eto kniga mamy._ — [ˈɛtə ˈknʲigə ˈmamɨ]

- **Это (this/it)** — pronoun.
    
- **книга (book, nom.)** — noun.
    
- **мамы (of mom, gen.)** — genitive shows possession.
    

**English:** _This is mom’s book._

---

### Example 3 — motion with case

**Русский:** **Я иду в школу.** — _Ya idu v shkolu._ — [ja ɪˈdu v ʂkʊˈlu]

- **иду (I go, on foot)** — verb.
    
- **в школу (into school, acc.)** — preposition + accusative (motion to).
    

**English:** _I’m going to school._

---

### Example 4 — questions

**Русский:** **Где твой дом?** — _Gde tvoy dom?_ — [gdʲe tvoj dom]  
**English:** _Where is your home?_

# 13. Common mistakes and how to avoid them

1. **Forgetting cases** — learn case endings with examples, not just lists. Practice with 5 nouns repeatedly.
    
2. **Stress errors** — stress changes pronunciation and sometimes meaning. Use dictionaries to learn stress.
    
3. **Mixing aspects** — remember perfective = completed action (use for single future), imperfective = ongoing/habitual.
    
4. **Wrong word order fear** — use word order to add emphasis; cases tell roles.

Week 1 — Alphabet, pronunciation, 50 common words, basics of gender and plurals.

Week 3 — Verbs: present, past; learn 20 verbs (10 impf./10 perf.) and practice conjugation.
