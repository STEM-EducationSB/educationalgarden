# SPANISH FULL GRAMMAR TUTORIAL

**(Beginner → Intermediate Foundation)**

## 1. **Basics of the Language**

- Spanish is a **gendered, inflected** Romance language.
    
- Word order: **SVO** (same as English):  
    Yo como arroz = I eat rice.
    
- Spanish has **agreement**: nouns, adjectives, articles change depending on **gender** and **number**.
    

---

# 2. **Articles**

Articles agree with the noun.

### **Definite (the)**

|Gender|Singular|Plural|
|---|---|---|
|Masculine|el|los|
|Feminine|la|las|

Examples:

- el libro (the book)
    
- la mesa (the table)
    
- los perros (the dogs)
    

### **Indefinite (a/an/some)**

|Gender|Singular|Plural|
|---|---|---|
|Masculine|un|unos|
|Feminine|una|unas|

---

# 3. **Nouns**

Spanish nouns are **masculine** or **feminine**.

### Common patterns:

- **-o → masculine**  
    el chico (boy)
    
- **-a → feminine**  
    la casa (house)
    

But some are exceptions:

- el día
    
- la mano
    

### Plurals:

- Vowel ending → add **-s**  
    libro → libros
    
- Consonant ending → add **-es**  
    ciudad → ciudades
    
- Ending in -z → change to **c + es**  
    lápiz → lápices
    

---

# 4. **Adjectives**

Adjectives **agree with the noun**.

### Basic forms:

- Masculine singular: **alto**
    
- Feminine singular: **alta**
    
- Masc. plural: altos
    
- Fem. plural: altas
    

If ending in **-e or -ista**, form does NOT change for gender:

- inteligente → inteligente
    
- futbolista → futbolista
    

Plural still adds **-s** or **-es**:

- inteligentes
    
- futbolistas
    

### Position:

Usually **after** the noun:  
el coche rojo (the red car)

But some common adjectives come **before**:

- buen / bueno (good)
    
- mal / malo (bad)
    
- gran / grande (big/great)
    
- nuevo (new)
    

**Meaning changes** depending on position sometimes:

- un gran hombre = a great man
    
- un hombre grande = a big man
    

---

# 5. **Pronouns**

### Subject pronouns:

|Spanish|English|
|---|---|
|yo|I|
|tú|you (informal)|
|él|he|
|ella|she|
|usted|you (formal)|
|nosotros/as|we|
|vosotros/as|you all (informal, Spain)|
|ellos/ellas|they|
|ustedes|you all (Latin America)|

Most of the time, **subject pronouns are dropped**:  
Hablo español (I speak Spanish).

---

# 6. **Verb Types**

Spanish has **three verb endings**:

- **-ar** (hablar)
    
- **-er** (comer)
    
- **-ir** (vivir)
    

We'll focus on **regular** patterns first.

---

# 7. **Present Tense (Regular Verbs)**

### **-AR**

hablar → to speak

|Person|Ending|Example|
|---|---|---|
|yo|-o|hablo|
|tú|-as|hablas|
|él/ella/usted|-a|habla|
|nosotros|-amos|hablamos|
|vosotros|-áis|habláis|
|ellos/ellas/ustedes|-an|hablan|

### **-ER**

comer → to eat  
| yo como | tú comes | él come | nosotros comemos | vosotros coméis | ellos comen |

### **-IR**

vivir → to live  
| yo vivo | tú vives | él vive | nosotros vivimos | vosotros vivís | ellos viven |

**Pattern trick:**  
AR → a  
ER → e  
IR → e  
Nosotros and vosotros behave similarly in ER/IR.

---

# 8. **Present Progressive (ing-form)**

Use **estar + gerundio**:

- -ar → -ando  
    hablar → hablando
    
- -er / -ir → -iendo  
    comer → comiendo  
    vivir → viviendo
    

Example:  
Estoy comiendo = I am eating.

---

# 9. **Past Tenses**

Spanish has two main pasts:

## 9.1 **Preterite** (Completed actions)

### Regular -AR:

| hablé | hablaste | habló | hablamos | hablasteis | hablaron |

### Regular -ER / -IR:

| comí | comiste | comió | comimos | comisteis | comieron |

Use it for:

- finished actions
    
- sequence of events
    
- interruptions
    

---

## 9.2 **Imperfect** (Ongoing past, descriptions)

### -AR:

hablar → hablaba, hablabas, hablaba, hablábamos…

### -ER / -IR:

comer/vivir → comía, vivía, etc.

Use it for:

- background
    
- habits
    
- age
    
- weather
    
- descriptions
    

**Trick:** Imperfect = “was/were ...ing / used to ...”

---

# 10. **Future**

### **Simple Future (regular)**

Just add endings to **infinitive**:

- **é, ás, á, emos, éis, án**
    

hablaré, hablarás  
comeré, comerás  
viviré, vivirás

---

# 11. **Conditional**

Add to infinitive:

- **ía, ías, ía, íamos, íais, ían**
    

hablaría, comería, viviría

Meaning: “would”

---

# 12. **Reflexive Verbs**

Actions done to yourself.

lavarse → to wash oneself

Pronouns:  
me, te, se, nos, os, se

Example:  
Me levanto = I get up.

---

# 13. **Ser vs Estar**

One of the biggest grammar pillars.

### Ser = permanent, identity, origin

Soy indio.  
Ella es alta.

### Estar = temporary, location, moods

Estoy triste.  
Madrid está en España.

---

# 14. **Por vs Para**

### Para = destination, goal, deadlines

Este regalo es para ti.

### Por = reason, travel, exchange

Voy por tren.  
Lo hice por ti.

---

# 15. **Direct & Indirect Objects**

### Direct Object Pronouns:

me, te, lo/la, nos, os, los/las

### Indirect Object Pronouns:

me, te, le, nos, os, les

Trick:  
Le/les → becomes **se** before lo/la/los/las.

Example:  
Se lo doy = I give it to him.

---

# 16. **Prepositions**

Common ones:

- en = in
    
- a = to
    
- de = of/from
    
- con = with
    
- sin = without
    
- sobre = on/about
    
- entre = between
    

---

# 17. **Question Structure**

Invert subject and verb:

- ¿Qué haces? = What do you do?
    
- ¿Dónde vives?
    

Common question words:

- qué = what
    
- quién = who
    
- cuándo = when
    
- dónde = where
    
- por qué = why
    
- cómo = how
    

---

# 18. **Negation**

Just add **no** before the verb:

- No hablo español.
    

Double negatives are normal:

- No veo nada = I see nothing.
    

---

# 19. **Comparatives**

más + adjective + que = more ... than  
menos + adj + que = less … than  
tan + adj + como = as ... as

Superlatives:  
el/la más + adj = the most...

---

# 20. **Commands (Imperative)**

Very common in daily Spanish.

### Tú affirmative:

- hablar → habla
    
- comer → come
    
- vivir → vive
    

### Tú negative:

- no hables
    
- no comas
    
- no vivas
    

Formal (usted):

- hable
    
- coma
    
- viva
    

---

# 21. **Subjunctive (light intro)**

Used for:

- wishes
    
- emotions
    
- doubts
    
- recommendations
    

Form:  
Take **yo form**, drop **-o**, add endings.

AR → e  
ER/IR → a

Example:  
Quiero que tú hables.  
Espero que vengas.

---

# 22. **Essential Sentence Patterns**

### 1. Yo + verbo + complemento

Hablaré español.

### 2. Verbo reflexivo

Me siento bien.

### 3. Estar + gerundio

Estoy estudiando.

### 4. Ir + a + infinitivo (near future)

Voy a aprender español.

---

# 23. **Memory Tricks**

- AR verbs feel more _open_ → use **a** sounds.
    
- ER/IR often behave the same.
    
- Conjugations are patterns, not memorisation. Compare columns visually.
    
- Keep pronouns inside the verb world:  
    lo veo, te digo, se lo doy.
