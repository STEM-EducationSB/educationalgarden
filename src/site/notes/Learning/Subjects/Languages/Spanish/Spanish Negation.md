# **SPANISH NEGATION – FULL GUIDE**

Spanish negation is actually very logical. The moment you understand the structure, you can handle any tense or sentence.

---

# **1. BASIC NEGATION: “NO” BEFORE THE VERB**

This is the foundation.  
Spanish doesn’t use “do/does/did” like English.  
You simply put **NO** **before** the verb.

**Formula:**  
**No + verb**

Examples:

|Spanish|English|
|---|---|
|No hablo español.|I don’t speak Spanish.|
|No como carne.|I don’t eat meat.|
|No tengo tiempo.|I don’t have time.|
|No voy a la escuela hoy.|I am not going to school today.|

This rule NEVER changes, no matter the tense.

---

# **2. NEGATION WITH TWO VERBS (Modal, Future próximo, etc.)**

When you have two verbs together, **“no” still goes before the FIRST verb**.

Examples:

|Spanish|Explanation|
|---|---|
|**No puedo hablar.**|“No” before “puedo” (first verb).|
|**No quiero comer.**|“No” before “quiero”.|
|**No voy a estudiar.**|“No” before “voy”.|
|**No tengo que hacer la tarea.**|“No” before “tengo”.|

---

# **3. NEGATIVE WORDS (Nadie, Nada, Nunca…)**

Spanish allows “double negatives.”  
This is normal and 100% correct.

### **List of all negative words**

|Spanish|Meaning|
|---|---|
|**no**|not|
|**nada**|nothing / anything|
|**nadie**|nobody / anybody|
|**nunca / jamás**|never|
|**ningún / ninguno / ninguna**|no / none|
|**tampoco**|neither / either|
|**ni… ni…**|neither … nor …|

---

# **4. HOW TO USE NEGATIVE WORDS**

You can use them in **two ways**:

---

## **A. Put “NO” + negative word later in sentence**

This is most common:

|Spanish|English|
|---|---|
|**No veo a nadie.**|I don’t see anybody.|
|**No quiero nada.**|I don’t want anything.|
|**No estudio nunca.**|I never study.|
|**No tengo ningún libro.**|I have no book.|

Pattern: **No + verb + negative word**

---

## **B. Negative word at the beginning (NO removed)**

If the negative word comes first → **no** disappears.

|Spanish|English|
|---|---|
|**Nadie viene.**|Nobody comes.|
|**Nunca estudio.**|I never study.|
|**Nada es fácil.**|Nothing is easy.|
|**Ningún estudiante llegó.**|No student arrived.|

This is also correct in A2.

---

# **5. DOUBLE NEGATIVES ARE NORMAL**

Spanish LOVES double negatives.  
You **must** use them.

Incorrect (in Spanish grammar):  
X _No veo nadie._

Correct:  
✓ **No veo a nadie.**  
✓ **Nadie veo.**

---

# **6. “NINGÚN / NINGUNO / NINGUNA” – THE TRICKY ONE**

### **NINGÚN** = masculine, before a noun

- Ningún problema
    
- Ningún libro
    
- Ningún estudiante
    

### **NINGUNO / NINGUNA** = stands alone

- No tengo ninguno.
    
- No tengo ninguna.
    

---

# **7. “TAMPOCO” (Neither / Not either)**

**Tampoco** works like “neither” or “me too but negative.”

Examples:

|Spanish|English|
|---|---|
|No quiero ir. — **Yo tampoco.**|I don’t want to go. — Me neither.|
|No estudio francés. — **Tampoco estudio español.**|I don’t study French. — I don’t study Spanish either.|

---

# **8. “NI… NI…” (Neither… nor…)**

Examples:

|Spanish|English|
|---|---|
|No tengo **ni** dinero **ni** tiempo.|I have neither money nor time.|
|Ni estudio ni trabajo.|I neither study nor work.|

---

# **9. NEGATION WITH PRONOUNS (lo, la, me, te, se…)**

The order is:

### **No + pronoun + verb**

Examples:

|Spanish|English|
|---|---|
|No lo sé.|I don’t know it.|
|No me habla.|He doesn’t talk to me.|
|No te veo.|I don’t see you.|
|No se lo doy.|I don’t give it to him.|

---

# **10. NEGATION IN PAST TENSE**

Works exactly the same:

|Spanish|English|
|---|---|
|No fui.|I didn’t go.|
|No comí.|I didn’t eat.|
|No hice nada.|I did nothing.|
|No vi a nadie.|I didn’t see anybody.|

---

# **11. NEGATION IN FUTURE TENSE (Simple & Próximo)**

### **Futuro Simple**:

No comeré (I will not eat)

### **Futuro Próximo** (IR + A + INF):

No voy a comer (I am not going to eat)

Always **no + first verb**.

---

# **12. NEGATION IN PERFECT TENSE (Present Perfect)**

Structure:  
**No + haber + participle**

Examples:

|Spanish|English|
|---|---|
|No he comido.|I have not eaten.|
|No hemos visto nada.|We haven’t seen anything.|
|No han llegado.|They haven’t arrived.|

---

# **Final Quick Summary (A2-Level)**

1. **No + verb**
    
2. **Negative words join happily:**
    
    - nadie, nada, nunca, tampoco, ningún…
        
3. **Double negatives required**
    
4. **“No + pronoun + verb”**
    
5. **No goes before the first verb**
